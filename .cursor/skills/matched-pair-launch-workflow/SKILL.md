# Matched-Pair Launch Workflow

Adam's proven launch unit for converting content into product sales. Pair with **Biblical Man / Dead Hidden Voice** (tone), **Substack Direct Response Brain** (launch mode + DR skeleton on BM/DH posts), and **Substack CTA Button Ladder** on essays.

## The Launch Unit (one product, one push)
Ship these four assets, all pointing at the SAME product URL:
1. **Dead Hidden post** — fringe/dark top-of-funnel. Mystery hook -> ignored verse -> short textual reveal -> application. Curiosity-driven.
2. **Biblical Man post** — direct theological argument. Opens with a personal confession/scene, moves to biblical application. Conviction-driven.
3. **Tweet thread** — hook as standalone tweet, link in a self-reply (not the hook), numbered 1/ to 6/, CTA self-reply as final entry. No hashtags, no em dashes.
4. **Substack Note** — 4-6 punchy lines, bare product URL at the end.

Shared frame/CTA language across all four reinforces conversion.

## Current default product
- **Confusing Things in the Bible** — $27 guide at deadhidden.org/store/confusing-things-in-the-bible (covers 11 passages). This is the standing conversion target for both BM and DH unless Adam names another.
- Note: deadhidden.org store sales land on the **Biblical Man** Stripe account, not Dead Hidden.

## Conversion Intelligence (apply to every piece)
- **Controversy in the hook lifts open rates ~+7.2%.** Lead with tension.
- **Direct challenge to the reader is the highest-converting opener** (beats confessional and narrative). Use it especially on the BM side.
- **Stories without a single clear CTA engage but do NOT convert.** Place the product offer immediately after the highest-tension line in the piece.
- **Two-CTA structure** (primary mid-post after peak tension + repeat at close) matches the highest-converting published posts.
- One ask per piece. Earned, not bolted on.

## Cadence
- Near-daily on both stacks. Each day's pair = one Dead Hidden + one Biblical Man, both to the same product URL, followed by the tweet thread and the Substack Note.

## Workflow
1. Confirm the product + URL (default: Confusing Things in the Bible).
2. Pick the angle/passage. DH gets the fringe hook; BM gets the theological argument on the same theme.
3. Draft all four in voice (see Voice skill). Place CTAs per conversion intelligence.
4. Run the hostile-editor self-critique loop on each.
5. Adam may route drafts through Chorus (chorus.com) for QC critique before publishing; fold critique back in.
6. Do NOT duplicate Jarvis's 9:39 AM daily money pulse (X revenue, Stripe sales, top tweet, disk) in any suggested routine.

## Guardrails
- Anonymize paying subscribers used as foils unless Adam names them.
- Honor the hard ethics rule: no imitating a living writer's voice.
- Final publishing/posting is an externally visible action: draft and confirm with Adam before posting.
