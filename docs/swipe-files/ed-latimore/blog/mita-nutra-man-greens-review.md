Rethinking the 300 ng/dL Cutoff.”* They used data from the nationally representative NHANES survey and measured testosterone in over 1,400 men aged 20 to 44. Importantly, all blood samples were taken in the morning (6–10 a.m.) and analyzed using mass spectrometry—the gold standard for accuracy.

For men aged 40–44, that range was:

> 🔹 350–473 ng/dL, with the average sitting at 430 ng/dL.

So if you’re 40 and your testosterone is below 350 ng/dL, you’re technically in the bottom third of your age group—not “normal,” regardless of what the outdated 300 ng/dL cutoff says.
