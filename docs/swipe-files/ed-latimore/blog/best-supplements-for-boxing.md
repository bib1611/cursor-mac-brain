Betaine isn’t as well-known as creatine, but it deserves a place in every fighter’s stack. At its core, betaine acts as an osmolyte—it helps cells stay hydrated and function under stress. For boxers grinding through weight cuts and endless sweat sessions, that hydration advantage is invaluable.

Beta-alanine raises your muscle carnosine stores, delaying that burn so you can keep your hands moving and your feet light, even when fatigue sets in. In fact, studies consistently show that beta-alanine supplementation improves muscle endurance, repeat sprint ability, and total work output—all directly relevant to boxing.

## **Pregnenolone**

FAQs About Boxing Supplements

**Are supplements allowed in boxing?**

- Yes, but fighters must follow World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) rules.
- Legal supplements include creatine, beta-alanine, citrulline, CoQ10, and most vitamins/minerals.
- Always use NSF or cGMP-certified brands to avoid contamination.

**Which supplements are banned by WADA?**

- Testosterone, anabolic steroids, prohormones, peptides, and DHEA/DHEA-S are prohibited.
- Even “milder” hormone boosters can trigger a failed test.
- Pregnenolone, colostrum, NMN, creatine, and betaine are currently WADA-compliant.

**What’s the best supplement stack for boxers?**

- *For power & endurance*: Creatine + beta-alanine (Mita Power).
- *For cardio & energy*: Citrulline + CoQ10 + shilajit (Mita Drive).

- *For recovery*: Colostrum + vitamin D3.

**Do I still need protein powder and electrolytes?**

- Yes — these are the basics.

- Electrolytes (sodium, potassium, magnesium) → essential during weight cuts and training in heat.

**What supplements should boxers avoid?**

- Anything banned by WADA (DHEA, SARMs, prohormones).
- Proprietary blends that hide dosing or include risky stimulants.
- Products without third-party testing (highest contamination risk).

**What’s the single most important supplement for boxers?**

- Power & explosiveness → creatine.
