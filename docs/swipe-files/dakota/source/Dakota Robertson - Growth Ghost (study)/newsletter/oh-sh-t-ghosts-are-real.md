WTF Is a Ghostwriter?

A ghostwriter is someone who pretends to be another person on the internet. 

But not some catfish on Tinder.

A ghostwriter takes over someone’s social media or newsletter and writes content under their name with the goal of either:

Increasing their followers

Making them money

Growing their email list

Ideally, doing all 3. 

And clients will pay quality ghostwriters quality prices.

Why People Pay Ghostwriters Thousands of Dollars a Month

There are 2 reasons people pay ghostwriters thousands of dollars a month:

Reason 1: 

Wealthy people already have money. 

What they can’t buy is fame.

So, wealthy will pay thousands of dollars per month to ghostwriters to grow their influence online.

It’s also a big d*ck measuring competition between their wealthy friends.

Reason 2: 

Money.

People who sell products and services have realized the power of social media and newsletters.

People like Gary V, Alex Hormozi, Grant Cardone have all built huge empires online.

It’s why professions like:

Consulting

Fitness coaching

Marketing services

Have exploded in popularity online.

By growing a social media following, you’re not just increasing the amount of attention on the stuff you’re selling, you’re also building trust.

Think about it, are you more likely to buy a product from a random person…

Or someone you know, like, and trust?

When you create content, you’re growing a trust-building asset for your client that can be turned into cash.

So if you, as a ghostwriter, make your client $10,000 in a month, are they going to be upset paying you $5,000?

Probably not.

Which means all these people need to capture attention online to get customers. 

And who are they going to turn to if they don’t know how to get attention or don’t have the time to get attention?

Those who can capture eyeballs and monetize them with words (AKA ghostwriters).

The Future Of Ghostwriting (And The Hidden Opportunity)

*Cue my grandpa voice*

“Back in my day when I started ghostwriting, there were only 3-5 ghostwriters.”

But as more people have learned about it over the past 3 years, more have slapped the label of “Ghostwriter” in their bio.

…and most of these people only have 57 followers. 

They can’t grow their own social media, let alone someone else’s.

And the same goes for writing newsletters.

It’s the equivalent of a fat personal trainer hitting you up asking if you want to lose weight.

So I don’t see ghostwriting getting saturated any time soon.

But I see a lucrative opportunity nobody is taking advantage of right now.

Becoming a Full-Stack Ghostwriter.

Sure, growing a social media following for a client is great.

But what if you could grow your client on multiple social medias?

What if you could turn those followers into email subscribers by writing a newsletter for your client?

What if you could turn those subscribers into customers by writing a sales page for your client?

Not only would you separate yourself from everyone else, you could charge 2x, 3x, or even 10x more.

Because the more money you make clients, the more they’re willing to pay.
