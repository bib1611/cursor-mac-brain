Sh*t Sandwiches, Anyone?

Mmm, just like momma used to make...

Dakota Robertson

May 5, 2025

Whether you’re trying to build a ghostwriting biz or an OF feet pic side hustle…

There’s 1 potato-level question that keeps you stuck: 

“Can I really do this?”

(I used to be one of these potatoes).

Back when I was flipping junk on eBay.

Back when I was doing graveyard shifts at Domino’s.

Back when I was hopping from failed business to failed business, wondering if something was wrong with me.

Amazon FBA?
Dropped it.

iPhone repairs?
Gave up.

Day Trading?
0 idea wtf was going on lol.

Each failure felt like more proof that I wasn’t cut out for this whole “entrepreneurship” thing.

Why? Because deep down I was still asking that same limp-d*ck question:

“caN I dO thiS?”

But when I stumbled into ghostwriting, something shifted.

I was fed up with my shitty wage, 0 location freedom, and watching people live out my dreams. 

So I finally asked a real question:

“Am I willing to do what it takes?”

Different question. Different outcome.

Wanna Eat Shit Sandwiches?

Back in 2020:

I had full-time college classes…

Worked graveyard shifts at Domino’s…

Hit the gym 5x a week (still had to look sexy)…

And I still showed up to write 5 tweets a day like my future depended on it.

Because it did.

No followers. No case studies. No experience.

Just delusion, desperation, and a refusal to live like a mediocre potato for the rest of my life.

So when I hear someone say:

“I don’t know if I can do this…”

I don’t coddle them.

I ask:

“Are you willing to eat shit sandwiches?”

(Figuratively…not literally, chill).

But are you willing to post when you have 0 followers?

Are you willing to work 2 extra hours a day to build your biz?

Are you willing to get ghosted 29 times and still show up for #30 like it’s your damn Super Bowl?

In short:

Are you willing to look “stupid” for a year while you figure things out?

Because when at the end of the day, lack of ability isn’t what holds most back. 

It’s lack of courage.

So if you’re not willing to pay the price of being “uncomfortable” for 1 year to be free for a lifetime, no worries, this ain’t for you.

But if you’re fed up with where you’re at in life like I was? 

Stop asking yourself “Can I…”

Start telling yourself “Let’s fucking go.”

Your Canadian friend,

Dakota “LFG” Robertson

He told himself he was willing to do what it takes.
