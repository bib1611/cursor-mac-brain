The 3 Pillar for Engaging Content

Before working together, Taylin was trying to be a broad self-improvement account for everyone.

This is a poor strategy because you don’t prove your competence.

If people think you’re incompetent, why would they follow or buy from you?

So, to separate himself from everyone else, I taught him my G-A-P Framework:

Tweet growth content (platitudes) to stay top of mind.

Tweet authority content (actionable advice + social proof) to show competence to potential clients.

Tweet personal content (stories + worldviews) to create relatability with your audience.

When writing tweets, use my “What, Why, How Framework.”

This simplifies your writing and triggers dopamine in the reader because it’s a complete thought.

1st part: state what you’re talking about.

2nd part: state why it’s important

3rd part: give actionable advice on how they can use it

How to Make Money From Ghostwriting

After I taught him the art of ghostwriting, I taught him the business.

First, we started with his offer.

We kept it simple:

• Targeted avatar

• A quantifiable result + benefits

• A specific time frame

• A risk reversal

Once he had his offer in place, he was ready to take on clients.

So, I sent leads his way and gave him a sales script as a guideline:

Find out why the prospect is on the call

Discover what problem they have, where they want to go, and ultimately, if you can help them achieve that

Overcome objections before they come up by asking questions

If you can help, show how your offer is the bridge between their current pain point to their desired result

State your price and stfu
