# GRADE-DAKOTA — the voice grader for blunt Dakota-register notes and tweets

This grades ONE thing: does it actually read like Dakota, or like a model doing a polite impression of him. It rides on top of GRADE.md (performance) and PART 1 (slop). Run it on any note or tweet that is supposed to be ballsy and blunt.

**The rule that took four tries to learn: GRADE AGAINST THE CORPUS, NOT YOUR IDEA OF HIM.** Open `Dakota/` and `swipe-files/dakota-ed-writing-dna.md` and match the texture before you grade. Your memory of Dakota is always too clean. The real one is meaner, shorter, and does not flinch.

---

## THE STANDARD (what passing sounds like)

Tweets are one punch. Brazen absolute, zero hedge, the cuss as the period.
- "Bet on yourself, motherfucker."
- "You're not the problem. The work is."
- "Using your balls will get you farther than your brain."

For Adam, the cuss-position converts to the hard short KJV-adjacent verdict, never softer:
- "You are not tempted. You are cheating. Use the word."
- "You are not brave. You are dramatic."

---

## THE FAILURE LADDER (the four drafts it took. Name the rung a draft is stuck on, then push to 4.)

1. **CLEAN / PREACHY.** Head-on "you do X" moralizing the reader saw coming. A sermon in note form. Not Dakota.
2. **ED, NOT DAKOTA.** Front-loaded verdict plus a shock coat of paint, but no scene, no self-deprecation, no bluntness as the punch. Closer, still wrong.
3. **TOO SOFT.** A real scene and real self-deprecation, but a tender confession with a parenthetical hug and the verse landing earnest. The structure is right and the balls are gone.
4. **PASS.** Ballsy and blunt. One punch. Brazen absolute. Verdict stated as fact. Verse lands like a closed fist. One short mean line to end.

Most misses are stuck at rung 1 (too clean) or rung 3 (too soft). Diagnose the rung first.

---

## THE CHECKS (falsifiable. Every box must pass.)

- [ ] **BLUNT.** The core claim is an absolute with the qualifier deleted. If "maybe / some of you / perhaps / I think / can" survived, it fails.
- [ ] **ONE PUNCH.** One idea, landed hard. If it argues two things, cut one.
- [ ] **DID-HE-JUST-SAY-THAT.** At least one line a polite Christian account would never post. If every line is safe, it fails.
- [ ] **CUSS-POSITION VERDICT.** The hardest, shortest line sits at the END of a beat as the punch, not buried mid-paragraph.
- [ ] **EXCUSE TURNED.** It takes the reader's own defense and uses it against him in one move ("a struggle has two men swinging. You quit years ago and started scheduling.").
- [ ] **FIST, NOT SERMON.** Scripture lands cold and mid-thought. Never "the Bible says," never explained after it.
- [ ] **MIC-DROP CLOSE.** Ends on a short, mean, closed line. "Use the word." "Sit down." "Bold." No tidy bow, no benediction.
- [ ] **NO HUG.** Zero tender parenthetical, zero softening aside, zero "I'm not preaching down at you" throat-clear. Self-deprecation is allowed ONLY as a fast blunt jab, never a sentimental confession.
- [ ] **TRUE + KJV-EXACT.** The blunt verdict is doctrinally sound and any verse is quoted exactly. Ballsy is not a license to lie. The edge sharpens the truth. It never invents it.

Pass = every box checked. One soft box means it is stuck on rung 3. Rewrite. Do not ship.

---

## SCENE EXCEPTION (newsletters only)

The blunt-tweet register above is for NOTES. A NEWSLETTER adds the corpus's other half: a real lived scene cold-open, self-deprecation, and a bait-and-switch (the "I got the worst STD" turning into "Shiny Thing Disorder" machine). The scene must be REAL, never staged. Notes punch. Newsletters tell the unhinged story first, then punch.
