WHY THIS GUIDE EXISTS

Amazon is not a "build it and they'll come" platform. It's a sophisticated marketplace that
rewards authors who understand its algorithms, invest strategically, and execute with
precision.

This guide distills the lessons learned from a successful launch and the expertise of former
Amazon employees into an actionable roadmap you can follow.



90 DAYS BEFORE LAUNCH: FOUNDATION PHASE

1. Publisher Setup

Register as a publisher on Amazon KDP (Kindle Direct Publishing). This gives you:

Full control over your brand presence

Access to advanced marketing tools

Professional author credibility

Action: Go to kdp.amazon.com and complete publisher registration



2. Brand Registry

Claim your brand through Amazon Brand Registry. Benefits include:

Intellectual property protection

A+ Content capabilities (enhanced product pages)

Advanced advertising options

Brand analytics dashboard

Action: Visit brandservices.amazon.com to register your brand
3. Category Research with Helium 10



Invest in Helium 10 (https://www.helium10.com/ ) or similar tools to research:

Which categories have high traffic but manageable competition

What keywords your target readers are searching

Where competitor books are positioned

Category trends and seasonality



Goal: Identify 3-5 categories where you can realistically reach Top 3

4. Create Your Amazon Author Page

Build out a complete author profile with:

Professional headshot and bio

Links to your website and social media

Video introduction (if possible)

Blog posts or articles showcasing your expertise

Action: Visit author.amazon.com to set up your page
60 DAYS BEFORE LAUNCH: REVIEW BUILDING PHASE



6. Distribute Advanced Reader Copies (ARCs)

Your goal: Get 10+ reviews before launch day. Send ARCs to:

Book bloggers and reviewers in your niche

Influencers and thought leaders in your space

Your most engaged email subscribers

Professional review services (like NetGalley)

Friends and colleagues (who will give honest feedback)



Warning: Never incentivize positive reviews. Always ask for honest feedback.



7. Set Up Amazon Advertising (Test Mode)

Begin running small test campaigns to learn what works:

Sponsored Products: Target competitor books (like Pete Hegseth's book)

Keyword Campaigns: Bid on high-intent search terms

Category Targeting: Advertise in relevant categories



Budget Guidance: Plan to spend 10-15% of projected revenue on ads



8. Create Additional Format Versions

Maximize revenue by offering multiple formats:

Kindle Edition: $9.99-$14.99 (discovery vehicle)

Paperback: $19.99-$24.99 (authority piece)

Hardcover: $26.99-$32.99 (premium gift option)

Audiobook: Consider ACX for Audible distribution
Pro Tip: Add exclusive content to Kindle (bonus chapter, extended foreword) to encourage
dual purchases. 10%+ of Kindle buyers will buy the physical copy.



9. Prepare Supplementary Products

Create additional revenue streams and deepen engagement:

Discussion guide (sell as PDF on Amazon or your website)

Workbook or study guide

Related curriculum materials

Companion resources
30 DAYS BEFORE LAUNCH: ACCELERATION PHASE



10. Optimize Your Product Page

Refine every element based on what you've learned:

A/B test different book descriptions

Add A+ Content (if Brand Registered)

Include high-quality interior preview images

Optimize your 7 backend keywords based on Helium 10 data

Add editorial reviews from ARC readers



11. Ramp Up Advertising Spend



Increase your daily ad budget significantly:

Double down on campaigns showing positive ROI

Pause underperforming keywords

Ensure you're bidding aggressively enough to reach Top 3 positions

Target multiple competitor titles simultaneously



Critical Insight: Only Top 3 search results get meaningful clicks. Your bids must be
competitive.



12. Set Up Fulfillment by Amazon (FBA)



If selling physical copies yourself:

Ship inventory to Prime-eligible Amazon facilities

Maintain adequate stock levels (stockouts kill momentum)
Monitor your seller account health daily



Why This Matters: Most customers filter for Prime. Prime eligibility is non-negotiable.



13. Build Your Email Launch Sequence



Prepare a series of emails to your list:

Pre-launch announcement (30 days out)

Behind-the-scenes content (20 days out)

Bonus content reveal (10 days out)

Launch day announcement with urgency

Follow-up asking for reviews (7 days post-launch)
LAUNCH WEEK: MAXIMUM MOMENTUM PHASE


14. Coordinate Multi-Channel Promotion



Create a synchronized push across all platforms:

Email blast to your entire list on launch day

Social media campaign with daily posts

Podcast appearances scheduled around launch

Guest blog posts on relevant sites

Partner promotions and cross-promotion

Press release distribution




15. Maximize Amazon Ad Spend



During launch week, increase budget to 15-20% of projected revenue:

Monitor campaigns hourly, not daily

Adjust bids based on real-time performance

Add new keyword variations as you discover them

Track your ad spend vs. sales ratio obsessively



Key Stat: 40% of book sales come from Amazon search ads. This is your primary discovery
mechanism.
16. Monitor Rankings Obsessively



Track your performance across multiple metrics:

Sales Rank: Updates hourly, shows immediate momentum

Best Sellers Rank: More stable, based on recent velocity

Category Rankings: Try to hit #1 in multiple categories

New Release Rankings: Your best chance at a badge




17. Leverage Social Proof



As reviews and rankings improve:

Screenshot bestseller badges and share on social media

Highlight positive reviews in your marketing

Create urgency around limited-time ranking achievements

Use ranking success to pitch media outlets
POST-LAUNCH: SUSTAINING MOMENTUM



18. Maintain Consistent Advertising



Don't pull back too quickly:

Reduce to sustainable 10% of revenue spend

Continue optimizing based on performance data

Test new keywords and competitor targets

Seasonal campaigns around relevant events




19. Request Reviews Systematically



Build toward 50, 100, and 500 review milestones:

Use Amazon's "Request a Review" button for every order

Follow up with purchasers via email (if you have their contact)

Continue sending ARCs to new reviewers

Engage with reviews (thank reviewers, address concerns)




20. Expand Your Catalog



Amazon rewards sellers who keep customers on platform:

Add supplementary products to your listings

Create product bundles

List related curriculum or resources
Consider a second book to build an author brand




21. Analyze and Iterate



Use data to improve continuously:

Track which keywords convert best

Monitor competitor movements in your categories

Test different pricing strategies

Experiment with Amazon promotions and deals

Review your sales funnel monthly

Kindle Edition: $9.99-$14.99 (discovery and volume play)



Paperback: $19.99-$24.99 (standard pricing for authority)



Hardcover: $26.99-$32.99 (premium positioning and gifts)

Pro Tip: Price Kindle aggressively for discovery, then capture higher margins on physical
formats. Remember: 10%+ of Kindle buyers will purchase the physical book if you give
them a reason.
COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

Waiting until launch day to list your book. You lose valuable preorder momentum and don't
have time to gather reviews.

Choosing categories based on prestige rather than winnability. Being #1 in a smaller
category beats being #500 in a large one.

Underspending on advertising. Amazon is pay-to-play. A $50/month ad budget won't move
the needle.

Ignoring the importance of Prime eligibility. Most customers filter for Prime-only products.

Stopping promotion after launch week. Sustained effort creates sustained results.

Not tracking metrics obsessively. You can't optimize what you don't measure.

Forgetting that reviews are social proof. Ten reviews is your first critical threshold.
