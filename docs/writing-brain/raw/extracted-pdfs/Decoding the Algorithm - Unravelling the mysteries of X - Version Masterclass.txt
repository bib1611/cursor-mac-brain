Now that we have taken a 10,000 feet view of how the algorithm works, here is a basic
breakdown of the process:

   1. The algorithm starts by scanning through approximately 100 million posts on the
      platform. From this massive pool, it identifies around 1,500 posts that it predicts
      you will find most enjoyable and engaging.

   2. Then, the system assigns an 'engagement probability score' to each of these
      selected posts. The posts are subsequently ranked from highest to lowest based
      on these scores.

   3. In the last step, the algorithm removes posts containing misinformation or
      originating from blocked accounts. It then curates your timeline by displaying the
      highest-scoring posts first, strategically placing advertisements every 2-3 posts.

      Note: This strategy is based on 2023 algorithm settings. The latest version of the
      algorithm may have undergone changes.



By understanding how X's algorithm selects, ranks, and filters posts, you can tailor your
content to enhance engagement. This knowledge can significantly boost your reach and
impact on the platform, helping you connect with a broader audience.

Decoding the X Algorithm

X's algorithm is a complex system designed to deliver the most relevant and engaging
content to its users. By analyzing various data points, such as the follower graph,
engagement metrics, and user behaviors, X curates a personalized feed for each user.
This process, known as candidate sourcing, involves filtering through hundreds of
millions of posts to select the top 1,500 that are most likely to be of interest.

Understanding how this algorithm works can help content creators optimize their posts
for better visibility and engagement on the platform.




                         Candidate Sourcing Flowchart (Part A)



X uses three types of data points:

          1) the follower graph showing who follows you,
          2) engagement data such as likes, reposts, and replies, and
          3) user data such as reposts, replies, mutes, unfollows, and spam reports.



This data is used to source relevant posts, a process known as candidate sourcing.

Candidate Sourcing



X attempts to extract the best 1,500 posts from a pool of hundreds of millions through
candidate sources.



This includes:


    1. People You Follow (In-Network)

        Roughly 50% or 750 posts come from here.

        In-network sources use a complex logistic regression model to filter out irrelevant
        posts and carefully curate the most important and timely messages from the
        users you follow.

        This happens through RealGraph, predicting the likelihood of two users
        engaging. The higher the score, the more likely they are to appear on your feed.
        The top posts go to the next level.




RealGraph constructs a weighted graph using details about a tweet, its author, and the intended recipient
to estimate the likelihood of interaction between them.

   2. People You Don't Follow (Out-of-Network)

      Roughly 50% or 750 posts come from here.

      X uses social graphs and embedding spaces to determine the relevance of posts
      from people you don't follow.

      The social graph considers what posts the people you follow have recently
      engaged with and who likes similar posts to you. These posts are then ranked
      using a logistic regression model called Graph jet.
      Embedding spaces work on content similarities, using numerical representations
      of users' interests and posts' content to calculate similarity.

             One of X's most useful embedding spaces is SimClusters, which discovers
             communities anchored by influential users using a custom matrix
             factorization algorithm.
             This helps posts reach those who are not already following you. There are
             145k communities, updated every three weeks, with users and posts
             represented in the space of communities.

Ranking System for Determination of Engagement Interest and Primary Ranking



X has 1,500 candidate posts that are probably relevant.



Scoring these posts directly predicts the relevance of each candidate's post and is the
primary signal for ranking posts on the timeline.


Variables contributing to a post's ranking include likes, reposts, replies, images, videos,
profile taps, X Blue, account reputation, unfamiliar words, unknown language, links,
misinformation, hashtags, and type of writing.


Your post's selection in the top 1,500 posts depends on these variables. The actions
and their weights are:

   •   Likes: Positive

   •   Reposts: Positive

   •   Replies: Positive

   •   Image & Video: Positive

   •   X Blue verified: Positive

   •   Good account reputation: Positive, Boosted.
   •   New words or unknown language: Negative
   •   Links: Negative ranking unless there is good engagement.
   •   Misinformation: Negative ranking.

   •   Multiple hashtags: Negative ranking.

   •   Type of writing: Negative ranking.

   •   Interacting with low-quality accounts and blacklisted topics: Negative ranking.

   •   Request "show less often" on your post or you, block/mute you, reports:
       Negative, this is terrible.



       Note: X could assign different weights to these and can change them at any time.

Appendix A: Steps to Maximize Reach

 1. Get X Blue:

    Subscribers get a boost in the algorithm if in the same network as the post's
    author and a smaller boost if not.


 2. Maintain a High Follower-Following Ratio:

    Reduces the page rank of users with a low number of followers but a high
    number of followings.
    The division factor based on this ratio reduces the user's page rank.



 3. Keep Your Tweepcred Higher than 65:

    Tweepcred is a score based on interactions, account age, followers, and device
    usage. Healthy engagement keeps it high, while spam reports, blocks, and mutes
    hurt it badly.



 4. Be active and consistent:
    Your posts should be recent.

    You should stick to a consistent schedule.
    You should make sure you stay on topic.



 5. Niche Down and Become a Trusted Voice:

    Become popular within a specific community by engaging with prominent
    accounts in that niche. This boosts visibility within that community.



 6. Post Threads or Long form posts:

    Informative posts or threads increase post engagement.
    Threads should have a good hook to reel in the audience.

7. Follow Influencers:

   Follow thought leaders and influencers in your area or industry.

   Engage with the content of these accounts.



8. Encourage your Followers to Engage.

   As discussed earlier, that engagement is a key factor in increasing the visibility of
   your posts.

   Pose questions and ask for responses.



9. Engage in the First 6 Hours:
   Posts have a half-life of 360 minutes. Engaging and replying to comments during
   this time can boost your post's relevancy.



10. Add Images & Videos:

   Posts with images and videos get a boost. Ensure the visual content is high
   quality and relevant.



11. Be Less Controversial:
   Avoid getting blocked, muted, or reported, as this reduces your "reputation
   score."



12. Create a Reason for Engagement:

   Content that encourages users to click, reply, and engage can skyrocket your
   reach. User actions are significant in this process.
