## Core thesis
Most people online stay creators. The ones who win become builders. Define your builder identity now so that in 90 days strangers DM you assuming a specific thing about what you are building. Position first, publish second.

## Key frameworks / rules
- **Creator vs Builder.** A creator makes posts. A builder is heading somewhere visible, and the audience can name the destination. Pick the destination on purpose.
- **The four-axis positioning grid.** Define yourself across four fields before you write more content:
  1. **World you build in** (the idea-space): faith, culture, masculinity, discernment.
  2. **Role you play in that world** (how people experience your voice): teacher, commentator, storyteller, investigator, guide, philosopher.
  3. **Content type you become known for**: essays, threads, articles, commentary, research, memes, interviews, tutorials. Pick one or two, not all.
  4. **What you are building toward** (the destination): newsletter, media brand, community, courses, events, full creator business.
- **The 90-day DM test.** Fill one blank: "In 90 days, strangers will DM me assuming that I am building ____." If you cannot finish that line, your positioning is not specific enough to attract the right inbound. This is the output the whole worksheet drives toward.
