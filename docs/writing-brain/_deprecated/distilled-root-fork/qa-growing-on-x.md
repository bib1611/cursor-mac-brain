## Core thesis
Quality over quantity wins under the current algo. Post a small number of strong things at the same time every day, then spend the rest of your hours replying inside your niche to compound reach. Consistency is a signal the algo reads. Take emotion out of guessing which post will hit, queue everything, and double down only after the data tells you what worked.

## Key frameworks / rules

- **Cadence by follower tier.** Under 1k and the 1k-10k range: post only 3 to 4 times a day, spaced at least 4 hours apart. Spend the rest of your allotted time replying to mentor accounts, not posting more.
- **Threads do not pay off until you have an audience.** Threads take hours to build. Below roughly 10k followers they often die in crickets, which is deflating and a poor use of hours. The closer to 10k, the more a thread can pop because organic follower engagement pushes it out-of-network.
- **Daily goal for a small account.** One high-performing reply a day that clears 50 or 100 likes. Make the reply the growth engine, not the post.
- **Two mentor lists.** List one is accounts in your niche. List two is adjacent niches you can add real context or commentary to. Mentor accounts do not have to be in your niche. Reply consistently for a couple of weeks, then DM. The "reply for weeks, then reach out" pattern turned one mentor relationship into a paid ghostwriting gig.
- **The "be so good they can't ignore you" play.** Reply daily to a target account even with something simple. They notice volume. They notice if you are subscribed. That visibility opens DMs and partnerships.
- **Pinned post = your highest performer.** New profile visitors check bio, photo, then the pinned post. Keep the single best post (proof: "8 million impressions") pinned so visitors see social proof before deciding to follow.
- **Never delete a weak post.** Leave everything up. A buried post can still pick up likes and reposts later, and a viral hit drives profile views where people scroll your older posts.
- **Keep your timeline clean of others' reposts.** Manually un-repost others every 2 to 3 hours (and automate it) so visitors scrolling your profile see your work, not borrowed content. Repost others inside replies and community, not on the public timeline.
- **Remove emotion from the queue.** Do not pre-judge a post as a "banger." You are always wrong about which one hits. Set an hour, create the posts, add them to the schedule, move on. Identify winners from the data, then create 1 to 2 similar posts a day until that vein runs dry.
- **End every reply and statement with a question.** Conversations inside your threads and posts are a strong engagement signal, so keep the dialogue going. Close replies with a question.
- **Pay to play: X Premium Plus over Premium.** Get the biggest tier. It is a visible algo boost and a credibility marker. People do not take un-verified accounts seriously. The cost is the price of admission.
- **Thread length cap ~10.** Keep the body to about 10 items max. A healthy thread shows ~36,000+ impressions on each internal post, proof readers are going past the hook all the way to the end.
- **Video on threads to claim the video tab.** Convert the two-panel thread hook graphic into a video instead of a static image. Any post with video gets featured on the mobile X video tab, an extra surface for reach.
- **Same time every day.** Post the daily thread at the same time daily (he runs ~4 a.m. Central). The algo reads the consistency and pushes proven, engaged content to more people.
- **Repurpose threads into Substack articles.** 11 months of daily threads becomes an endless article bank. Each old thread converts into one Substack article (system-driven). The daily-thread habit feeds the Substack engine for free.
- **Per-platform flavor, not copy-paste (at scale).** Early on, repost the same content as Substack Notes the same way. Once you have bandwidth, give each platform its own format. Free Substack newsletters become X articles. X posts become Notes.
- **WriteStack note categories with guardrails.** Take a proven note-format list (from WriteStack's creator), write your own 10 to 12 examples by hand, then use AI for more examples inside those exact categories. Posting only in those formats produced more restacks and likes over 3 weeks.

## Hook or thread patterns

- **The "name it, I'll show it" audience prompt.** Ask followers to name a place (or thing) and you reply with a photo or video of it. Runs about every two weeks. Builds a long reply chain, strong impressions, and a backlog of post ideas. Plays well even with joke answers.
  - Brand-adapted (KJV discernment): "Name the verse your pastor skips. I'll tell you why he skips it." Then reply down the chain, one verse at a time. Same mechanic, scar-tissue payoff.
  - Brand-adapted: "Drop the one sin you can't kill. I'll give you the KJV verse that names it." A reply chain that converts lurkers into commenters.
- **Two-panel thread hook turned into video.** Hook line on top, payoff promise below, rendered as a short video so it lands on the video tab. Travel example hook: "Bora Bora is beautiful enough to make almost anyone want to go. The prices? Here are 10 cheaper alternatives that still deliver the dream island feeling."

## Brand application
- Run the cadence honestly. The Biblical Man and Follow Me are not at the under-10k tier, so threads are live. But the discipline holds: 3 to 4 strong posts a day spaced out, then hours of replies on discernment and men's accounts. Replies are the growth engine, not volume.
- Pin the cultural grenade. Keep your single highest-impression confrontation pinned so every man who lands from a viral hit sees proof before he decides to follow.
- Run the reply-chain prompt twice a month. "Name the verse your pastor skips. I'll tell you why." It builds comment volume (the +20 signal), feeds you a backlog of post ideas, and ends every reply with a question by design.
- Build two mentor lists. One in-niche (discernment, KJV, men's accounts). One adjacent (history, marriage, classical-Christian). Reply daily for weeks, then DM. That is how partnerships and quote-recommendations get built.
- Turn one thread a day into one Follow Me or Biblical Man post. The thread bank is the article bank. Stop writing twice.
- Get Premium Plus and keep the timeline clean. Un-repost others off the public profile. A discernment reader judges you in the first three posts he sees.
- Kill the "this one's a banger" instinct. Queue the draft, publish, let the data name the winner, then write 1 to 2 follow-ups in that vein until it dies.
