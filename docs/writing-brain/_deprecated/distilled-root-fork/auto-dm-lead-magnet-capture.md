## Core thesis
Pair one engagement-bait tweet with an automatic DM that delivers a free guide. Anyone who likes or replies gets a DM with the download link. The download requires an email, so a single tweet quietly harvests thousands of emails on autopilot while it also farms the algorithm's top signals.

## Key frameworks / rules
- The capture loop: post tweet, reader likes or replies, auto-DM fires with the link, link goes to a gated free guide, reader trades email for the guide. You get the email, they get the lift.
- Set the trigger to the easiest action. Accept like OR reply. Lower the bar so more people qualify and the funnel fills.
- The DM must match the tweet's promise line for line. List the exact benefits in the tweet, then repeat the same benefits in the DM so the reader knows precisely what they are getting. Mismatch kills follow-through.
- Add urgency to force action. "Link valid for 24 hours" makes people click now instead of later.
- Gate the campaign behind account credibility. Only run auto-DM once you are verified, have two-factor on, and have run the page a while. A cold or unverified account should not do this.
- Cap volume at 500 DMs per campaign. X shuts the DM faucet off past heavy automated sends, so 500 is the safe ceiling.
- When the cap hits, switch the tweet to a manual call-to-action so the post keeps converting after automation stops.
- A free, named lead magnet still earns money. Host the free guide on a storefront (Gumroad) under a real product name at a free price, and back-end sales come off the same page.

## Hook or thread patterns
The tweet that powers this is a proof-and-promise hook with an engagement command tacked on. Pattern from the transcript:

"Three years ago: 5,000 followers, zero dollars in online sales, zero engagement. Today I have [result]. I quantified the system into a free course that teaches you the same. Like and comment [trigger word] and it's yours."

Brand-adapted (KJV discernment), do not put the link in the body, deliver it by DM:
- "Ten years ago I taught Sunday school and could not defend one verse under fire. I wrote a free guide: the 12 verses your modern translation gutted, and how to answer for them. Reply SWORD and it is yours."
- "I read Revelation forty times before I saw it. I put the one word every pulpit skips in a free guide. Like and reply WATCH. I will send it to you."

The DM that follows must restate the same promise:
- "Thank you for replying. Here is your free guide on the 12 gutted verses. [link]. It will show you exactly which words were cut and how to answer. Link valid for 24 hours."

## Brand application
This is the missing back end for every tweet that already pops. The discernment posts that draw a flood of replies are leaking. Right now those readers like, argue, and scroll on. With auto-DM, every one of them becomes a chance to land on the email list, which Adam owns and cannot get banned off of.

Do it the brand way. The lead magnet is already built (the Plain Bible Manual sampler, a gutted-verses one-pager, a Christie mini-vault piece). Gate one behind email. Write the tweet as confession-plus-proof, not as a guru flex. The trigger word should fit the voice (SWORD, WATCH, AMEN), never "DM me." Keep the link out of the body so the algorithm does not throttle it. The DM pays off the exact promise and runs out in 24 hours.

Guardrails that fit Adam's account: he is verified, has two-factor, has a long-standing 52.9K-follower page, so he clears the credibility gate. Cap at 500. When it caps, flip the post to a self-reply link. Watch the block-and-report risk noted in PART 2; a confrontational tweet that also auto-DMs strangers can draw spam reports, so tie the bait to a genuine value drop, not pure rage.

One honesty note: the gate must deliver. A discernment reader who trades an email for a thin PDF never opens the next one. The guide has to be worth the address.
