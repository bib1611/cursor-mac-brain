# Amazon Book Launch Playbook

## Source
- File: `Masterclass26 How To Launch on Amazon (1).txt`
- Teacher: Robert B (X.com/TheRobertBShow), built from a launch that hit #78 in Amazon New Releases plus advice from former Amazon employees
- Topic: step-by-step KDP / Amazon bestseller launch
- Type: pdf

## Core thesis
Amazon is pay-to-play, not "build it and they come." A book wins by ranking inside winnable categories, stacking reviews before launch day, and buying its way to the top 3 of search with ads, then sustaining the spend so visibility compounds into rank into more visibility.

## Key frameworks / rules
- Four-phase timeline. 90 days out (foundation), 60 days out (reviews), 30 days out (acceleration), launch week (max momentum), then post-launch (sustain).
- 90 days out: register on KDP, claim Brand Registry (unlocks A+ Content and brand analytics), research categories with Helium 10, build an Author Central page.
- Category rule: pick 3 to 5 categories where you can realistically reach Top 3. Winnable beats prestigious. #1 in a small category beats #500 in a big one.
- 60 days out: get 10+ honest reviews via ARCs (book bloggers, niche influencers, most-engaged subscribers, NetGalley, honest friends). Never incentivize positive reviews.
- Multi-format ladder for one book: Kindle as the discovery vehicle, paperback as the authority piece, hardcover as the premium gift.
- Kindle exclusive-content move: add a bonus chapter or extended foreword to the Kindle so buyers also grab the physical book. 10%+ of Kindle buyers will buy the physical copy.
- Supplementary products: discussion guide, workbook, study guide, companion resources, each sold as its own listing or PDF.
- 30 days out: A/B test the book description, add A+ Content, add editorial reviews from ARC readers, optimize the 7 backend keywords with Helium 10 data.
- Prime is non-negotiable. Most buyers filter for Prime-only. Use FBA, watch stock daily, stockouts kill momentum.
- Ad-spend schedule as a percent of projected revenue: 10 to 15% in test phase, 15 to 20% in launch week, then settle back to a sustainable 10% post-launch.
- Launch week: monitor campaigns hourly not daily, monitor rankings hourly, screenshot bestseller and New Release badges as social proof.
- Review milestones to build toward: 10, then 50, then 100, then 500. Use Amazon's "Request a Review" button on every order.
- Catalog expansion: bundles, related curriculum, then a second book to build an author brand. Amazon rewards sellers who keep customers on-platform.

## Numbers and proof
- The teacher's own launch reached #78 in Amazon New Releases.
- Goal: 10+ reviews before launch day.
- 10%+ of Kindle buyers will buy the physical copy if given a reason.
- 40% of book sales come from Amazon search ads.
- Only the Top 3 search results get meaningful clicks.
- Ad spend: 10 to 15% (test), 15 to 20% (launch week), 10% (sustain) of projected revenue.
- Pricing: Kindle $9.99 to $14.99, paperback $19.99 to $24.99, hardcover $26.99 to $32.99, discussion guide $7.99 to $12.99.
- Review thresholds: 10, 50, 100, 500.
- A $50/month ad budget will not move the needle.
- Launch-week target: Top 100 in New Releases overall, #1 in at least one category, Top 3 for primary keywords.

- Supplementary products (discussion guide, study guide) fit Sunday-school-teacher Adam exactly. A church-group discussion guide priced $7.99 to $12.99 is a natural rung.
