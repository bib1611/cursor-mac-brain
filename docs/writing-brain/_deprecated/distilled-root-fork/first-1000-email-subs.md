# First 1,000 Email Subscribers

## Source
- File: `transcripts/YOUR_FIRST_1000_EMAIL_SUBS_.txt`

- Topic: building an email list off X from zero to 1,000 subs using lead magnets and auto-DM tweets
- Type: transcript

## Core thesis
On social you rent your audience. On an email list you own it. The fastest way to build that list is to run lead-magnet tweets paired with auto-DMs, so one tweet grows your following, builds trust, earns ad revenue, and collects emails at the same time. The list, not the timeline, is where you sell.

## Key frameworks / rules
- **Rent vs own.** Followers belong to the platform (Elon). Email is the one audience you own and can move between providers. No list means no backup if you get hacked, locked out, or banned.
- **Don't sell to the timeline.** Direct sells on the feed get suppressed by the algo and read as ads. Build relationships at the top of the funnel. Sell from the email list, not the post.
- **Start collecting emails early.** Begin promoting your list around your first 500 to 1,000 followers, not after you are "big."
- **One-problem lead magnet rule.** A lead magnet solves ONE big problem. Magnets that solve one problem outperform and out-earn magnets that solve three. Keep it short, high quality, one clear win. It is a first touch point, not a course.
- **The lead magnet's job is to lead.** Its real purpose is to route the reader to an offer, build trust, or make them aware of what you do, even when nobody buys.
- **Free + tip-enabled magnets still make money.** Priced at $0 with tipping on, they collect cash directly AND feed higher-ticket offers.
- **Workflow / automated follow-ups.** After a download, send subsequent automated emails that remind them to read the magnet and upsell the next offer.
- **Auto-DM requires a follow.** The auto-DM only fires if the person follows you, so the tweet must say "follow me + like/reply" to get it. This converts engagers into followers as a side effect.
- **Run the auto-DM tweet about once a week.** Not daily. It is a repeatable weekly engine.
- **Always download/export your subscriber file.** Keep exporting emails so you can move providers later. Ownership is the point.
- **Every space has a call to action.** Never host an X Space (or talk in a community) without pitching the list, a magnet, or an offer. Your time is the cost.
- **Get creative and be aggressive.** Stack capture points: viral-tweet CTAs, landing page in bio, Spaces, sharing wins in communities. Repetition (see, see, see) is the tactic.

## Platform choices (tooling, stated tradeoffs)
- **Gumroad:** lowest friction, upload a PDF in five minutes, supports tipping and workflows. Con: ~10% fees. Use it to start; host higher-ticket offers elsewhere if you want.
- **ConvertKit:** for the serious, competitive pricing, first 500 subs free. Start on Gumroad and migrate to ConvertKit later.
- **MailChimp:** priced near ConvertKit, only edge is prettier emails. AOP shrugs: "you win with words," so aesthetics matter little.
- **Substack caution:** good only if you have a huge account and will NEVER sell anything but content. Per AOP, you cannot upsell people already paying for premium, which kills funnel flexibility. (Note for Adam: this is AOP's 2023-era claim and may be stale; treat as dated.)
- **Lead-magnet capture tools:** Hype Fury or Tweet Hunter to automate the DMs. Can be done manually, just more work.

## Hook or thread patterns (lead-magnet tweet templates)
- **The capability-stack hook (the offer-tweet open):** "What if you could write a tweet that got massive engagement, built trust, made money, AND collected emails all at once? Would you be interested?" Open loop, then deliver the method.
- **Problem-named lead-magnet tweet:** state the platform truth, name the magnet, list the exact problems it solves, then give one instruction. Example structure: "X is the future of social media. I wrote a free guide on how to set up your profile, hit 10,000 followers, and sell offers. To get it: 1) follow me 2) like + reply 'X'."
- **The 40K-vs-free framing (from "get paid writing," 1,000 emails on one tweet):** "Writing is the most powerful skill in the world. Spend 40K learning it badly in college, or follow me and learn it free. Retweet this and I'll DM you a free guide." Anchor a high price against your free thing.
- **Auto-DM body format:** describe what it is, drop the link, add 24-hour urgency, then tell them to get off their butts and download it. "Here's your free guide. Here's what you'll learn. Link below. Good for 24 hours."
- **Post-thread CTA:** at the end of a viral thread, "hundreds of people have already signed up," then the list link. Lower quality than the targeted magnet tweet (more tire-kickers) but fine for early volume.
- **KJV-discernment brand adaptation of the named-magnet tweet:** "Most men can't defend the King James and they don't know why. I wrote a free guide: 7 verses the modern versions quietly changed. To get it: 1) follow me 2) reply 'KJV'." Pays off the hook with the exact verses, then routes to the Vault.
- **KJV adaptation of the price-anchor hook:** "You'll spend $40K on a seminary that teaches you the originals are lost. Or follow me and learn to defend your Bible for free. Reply 'sword' and I'll send the guide."

## Brand application
- Email is the missing leg of Adam's stack. Run a weekly lead-magnet tweet on @Biblicalman with an auto-DM. One tweet feeds the list, the following, and the Vault funnel at once.
- Build one-problem KJV magnets, not another mega-resource. "7 verses the modern versions changed." "The one prayer God refused to answer." "How a good father raised kids who left the faith." Each solves one problem and lands warm readers on a paid offer page.
- Make the entry magnet free with tipping on. It collects cash and emails before anyone hits the $7 sampler.
- Stop relying only on Substack capture. Substack owns that relationship and limits upsells. Mirror the list into a provider Adam exports and owns (Gumroad to start, then a real ESP), so the Vault / field-file ladder can be sold by email, not just the timeline.
- Every Space, every viral Note, every thread ends with one capture CTA. No naked posts. Be aggressive, repeat the offer.
- After download, fire an automated follow-up: confirm the win, then point to the next rung.
