# Substack Content Strategy — Multi-Platform Streamline

## Source
- File: `transcripts/CONTENT_STRATEGY.txt`
- Teacher: Substack-growth masterclass host (unnamed in clip), speaking to the Masterclass 24/7 community (Art of Purpose present). References Sahil Bloom and Dan Koe.
- Topic: How to run Substack, X, and Instagram together without more work, by making Substack the home base.
- Type: transcript

## Core thesis
Make Substack your primary hub and the other platforms become less work, not more. Write a daily brain-dump article, post slop on X for reach, and use every channel to feed people into the one asset you own. Every email you write exists to sell.

## Key frameworks / rules
- **Substack as home base, not just a channel.** One month in: published an article or email every single day. Daily writing on Substack saves time because all other content flows down from it.
- **Start wide, flow down (Sahil Bloom / Dan Koe model).** Make the biggest piece first, then everything else is chopped from it. Koe writes one long weekend article, turns it into a video, then chops it into social posts. The host runs a hybrid: writes daily because he has the chops.
- **Brain dump, then heavily edit.** Every article is a brain dump that gets cut down. Readers are reading an edited brain dump. One good tweet becomes the seed of a full article ("blow up this one idea").
- **Article skeleton that writes itself.** Intro, then an announcement or CTA at the very top if it expires soon, then the story broken into labeled sections, then a conclusion that draws the lesson, then the sell. The first-million / second-million / third-million article took 20 minutes with this frame.
- **Every email exists to sell.** The point of pouring heart into stories and lessons is to land "I have this for you." Give value, then make the ask.
- **The free/paid cadence (4 to 5 free + 1 paid per week):**
  - Friday: nuts-and-bolts, numbers, statistics, study-driven ("how I made my first million").
  - Sunday: more personal, curiosity-driven, no money or numbers ("the day I became a millionaire," "the business of being Christian"). Sunday is the highest sales day, so release the most interesting, most curious topic Sunday.
  - The four free types to rotate: numbers/findings piece, curiosity-driven reframe, personal overcoming story, benefit-driven how-to.
  - One impromptu email per week, paid or free, sourced from the group chat.
  - Paid drops roughly once a month: a recording, case study, old masterclass highlight, or coffee-call recording (released ~4 days later).
- **Write what is top of mind, not by rigid day-rules.** The cadence is a guide, not a cage.
- **Long library kills churn.** A big back-catalog of 20-minute to 1-hour recordings raises retention. Short-articles-only invites one-month-and-bail. People stay when they see a deep library they have not finished.
- **Mine your own chat for content.** The group chat and audience questions generate article topics. The system perpetuates itself: chat feeds articles, articles feed chat.
- **X is for slop and reach. Substack is for value.** Post wide viral slop on X, post depth on Substack. Take X far less seriously and have fun. ("This is the way the culture has been doing it for a year already.")
- **X ad revenue is a side effect, not the reason.** It is one of ~10 revenue drivers, not the driver.
- **Cross-post and recommendations as a growth lever.** Getting quote-recommended / cross-posted by a bigger creator brings overnight subscribers. Get others recommending you too.
- **Lead magnet builds the email list.** The hardest part is the first 1,000 email subscribers, not converting free to paid. A free lead magnet is how he built the list.
- **Profile-as-storefront audit.** Before blaming growth, fix the page. Banner, custom profile picture, clear promise of what the reader gets, matching aesthetic. A cheap uncropped banner signals "do not pay this person." Position the bio around what the reader wants, not your credentials. Never use insider acronyms nobody can decode (a "DUK DIG FC member" badge means nothing; "NBA, Harvard" would convert). Unrecognized social proof reads as no social proof, and people walk.
- **Niche width must match the platform's audience.** Too narrow a niche ("soccer coaches who care about growth purpose") has nobody in the room. Reframe the same expertise toward a broad want the reader already has (wealth, human dynamics, becoming the best version of yourself).

## Hook or thread patterns
- **The X-to-Substack swap.** On reposted/copied X content, where the subscribe button would normally sit, drop a curiosity CTA instead: "You are reading this on X. Click below for the full Substack." Stronger version: "Most of my takes on Substack are too hardcore for here. Click to access the full library." This single swap pulls X readers onto Substack.
  - Brand-adapted (KJV discernment): "You are reading the soft version on X. The post that names the pastor is on Substack. Read it below."
- **Paste a paid paragraph as a free X post.** Pull the single most interesting paragraph or quote from a paid article, post it raw on X as a curiosity tidbit. Watch what readers already quote you on, then feed those exact lines back as bite-size posts.
- **Old winners as new Notes.** Repost tweets that performed years ago (one got "23 likes" then, fresh to the new Substack audience now) plus old memes you never posted, as Notes. Thank people who shared.
- **Numbers-reveal post for trust.** "How I made my first million, my second million, and how I will make my third on Substack" structure: name each milestone, name the lesson behind each (discipline, then relationships, then relationship capital), close on the meta-lesson.
  - Brand-adapted: "The three times God broke me. What each one cost. What each one taught."

## Brand application
- Make Substack the hub for both The Biblical Man and Follow Me. Write the daily 4 AM brain-dump article, then heavily cut it. The Brutal Honesty Protocol already wants content over infra; this hands you a 20-minute article frame so the writing is the work.
- Run the cadence with the brand register: Friday gets the nuts-and-bolts discernment teardown (KJV vs modern versions, named doctrine, receipts). Sunday gets the 2 AM scar-tissue Follow Me piece (no numbers, pure confession and curiosity), since Sunday converts.
- Treat X as the sword's loud room. Post the hard slop for reach. Where the subscribe button would go, swap in the curiosity CTA to the Substack ("the post too hardcore for X is below").
- Paste single paragraphs from the paid Vault material as free X tidbits. Watch which lines readers quote, then feed those back.
- Build the email list with a lead magnet (a short KJV discernment field file), because the first 1,000 emails are the real wall, not free-to-paid.
- Stack the back-catalog deep (long Vault recordings, archived posts) so paid readers see they have not finished, which holds churn.
- Audit the profile before blaming reach: banner, picture, one clear promise to spiritually-dead men, no insider church acronyms a cold reader cannot decode.
- Land 3 to 5 discernment-adjacent creators for cross-posts and quote-recommendations.
