# How to Give Comments (the reply/comment growth engine)

## Source
- File: `transcripts/How_to_Give_Comments_.txt`
- Teacher: Dino (Art of Purpose), Create 24/7 / Create Publish Profit on X
- Topic: giving comments and replies as a daily growth, networking, and content-mining engine
- Type: transcript

## Core thesis
Giving 20 to 25 real comments every morning, in about 15 to 20 minutes, is one of the biggest levers a small account has. It does three jobs at once: it pushes your account algorithmically, it builds the relationships that turn into DMs, collaborations, and money, and it mines tomorrow's content off the timeline. No downside.

## Key frameworks / rules

- **The three reasons to comment daily (the whole system).**
  1. Algorithmic push. The more you comment, and the more the main creator engages back (likes, replies), the more they and their audience see your stuff. Reciprocity follows: they visit your page, see an active account, and comment back.
  2. Networking. Comments build a connection with another account. His pattern: comment on their account, they comment on yours, the conversation moves to DMs, DMs lead to collaboration, collaboration leads to money. If you never leave replies, you never make the connection in the first place.
  3. Content mining. The timeline tells you what people want to talk about today. Bookmark the good ones and reverse-engineer them into your own posts, emails, and sales emails.

- **Daily dose.** About 20 to 25 comments, roughly 15 to 20 minutes, every single morning. Framed as Ben Franklin's 15 minutes with the newspaper, swapped for 15 minutes reading the timeline.

- **Comments are currency.** Weighted almost as heavy as retweets, especially for out-of-network reach. A good comment from an out-of-network account can boost your page as much as a retweet.

- **The lazy-man method (default).** Open the For You page, find a post, turn on voice-to-text, talk out a reply that adds to the conversation, lightly edit, post. Fast enough to clear 20+ before the day starts.

- **What a great comment does.** Adds something to the conversation. Tells a bit about yourself and why you believe it. Format he uses out loud: "Good post, man. Here's what I would add to this one."

- **What kills a comment (do not do these).**
  - Don't upstage or attack the creator ("you don't know what you're talking about").
  - Don't link to your own posts in someone else's replies. He calls it "so annoying."
  - If a good reply doesn't come to the top of your head, skip the post. Don't force it.

- **The VIP-list workflow.** Build an X list of the accounts you want to engage daily: friends, peers, big accounts, and clients. Scroll the list chronologically and leave replies. This is more reliable than the For You page, which is often dead ("the For You page just really isn't that great").

- **XPro (advanced).** Found under X Premium, desktop only (former TweetDeck). Run columns side by side: notifications, VIP list, home, DMs. Lets the big accounts who run their page like a business see everything at once and reply fast. Pair XPro with the VIP list for the strongest version of this.

- **Comment chronologically and early.** Reply to fresh posts so your comment sits at the top, gathers likes and engagement, and gets seen. Don't comment on old posts. The one exception: a post that goes mega-viral a day or two later.

- **The reply-back triage (when your own comment section is flooded).** With 484 comments he will not answer 484. He ranks:
  1. Subscribers, tight friends, inner circle. Always reply.
  2. Blue checks making good comments. Reply.
  3. There is no number three. Non-blue-checks get buried; he does not work down to them.
  Big accounts (his size, 100k+ followers) that reply to you: never leave it hanging, always reply back. Same for a 200k account you want a deeper connection with.

- **The straw-man / "feed it to the tribe" move (his "secret sauce").** Find the dumbest or most hostile comment in your replies, the one your audience will eat alive, and reply back to it on purpose. Your reply lifts a buried non-blue-check comment up where everyone sees it, the hostile commenter replies, your tribe piles in, and the pile-on stacks engagement on your post. Routinely target the stupid comments and use them to your advantage. Contrasted with blocking dissenters, which he calls a mistake.

- **Reverse-engineer content while you scroll.** Bookmark posts that work and study the psychology. Example he flagged: a "humble flex" post (retired my wife 23 years ago, here's the hard part and what I learned) where the value is the lesson, not the brag. He turned two timeline posts that morning into his own content ideas, including a candidate sales email.

## Hook / thread / reply patterns

- **The add-to-it reply.** "Good post, man. Here's what I would add." Then your own angle. KJV/discernment version: under a man's post about leading his home, "Good word. Here's what I'd add. I read the Book before I read my phone now. Changed the whole house."

- **The story-graft reply.** Take the creator's frame and lay your own story over it. He saw "I retired my wife 23 years ago" and replied with his own: retired his, wife homeschools and runs the household, learning curve, best decision he made. Brand version under a marriage or provision post: a flat line of your own lived experience, no preaching down.

- **The straw-man bait reply.** Quote-lift a hostile reply by answering it so your audience can answer it for you. Brand version: under a comment calling Scripture a "mind virus," a calm one-liner that hands the thread to your tribe to defend the Book.

- **The humble-flex study.** Bookmark posts that flex then teach (hard thing happened, here is what I learned). The brag earns attention, the lesson earns the share. A repeatable shape for Follow Me scar-tissue posts.

## Numbers and proof

- ~20 to 25 comments per morning, ~15 to 20 minutes.
- Comments weighted "almost as heavy as retweets," strong for out-of-network reach.
- He had 484 comments on one post and triaged instead of answering all.
- One dumb comment he baited had ~150 likes; replying to it pulled a pile-on onto his post.
- A networking chain (Zubi) went comment, comment-back, DM, collaboration, money, and a follow from a big account.
- Two timeline posts that single morning became content ideas for him, one a possible sales email.

## Brand application (in Adam's voice)

Fifteen minutes. Same as a man reading his Bible before the house wakes.

Open X. Twenty replies. Not "amen." Not "great word." A line that adds something. A line that tells them who you are.

Voice-to-text. Talk it out. Move on.

Don't argue under the post. Don't drop your link. Add to it. "Good word. Here's what I'd add." Then bleed one true line.

Build a VIP list. The men you want in your corner. Big accounts. Friends. Reply to them every morning, oldest first, so you sit at the top.

Your own replies get flooded. Don't drown. Subscribers first. Inner circle first. Then the blue checks worth answering. There is no third list.

And the angry one calling the Book a mind virus? Don't block him. Answer him calm. Watch the men pile in. He just built your post for you.

Then bookmark the three posts that hit. Those are tomorrow's tweets. One of them is a sales email.
