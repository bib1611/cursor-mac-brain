# Q&A — Everything You Need to Know About Growing on X

## Source
- File: `Q&A -- Everything You Need to Know About Growing on X.txt`
- Teacher: Eric ("the time traveler" / The Traveler account), masterclass host. References Culture Explorer's algo lesson, Orel (creator of WriteStack), Dino, Tim Denning.
- Topic: live operational Q&A on growing an X account — posting cadence, threads, replies, mentor accounts, premium tier, cross-posting to Substack.

## Core thesis
Quality over quantity wins under the current algo. Post less, reply more, and earn the algo's trust with consistency (same content type, same time, every day). Bookmarks are the engagement signal the algo rewards most.

## Key frameworks / rules

- **Small-account cadence (under ~1,000, or 1,000–10,000 followers):** Post 3–4 times a day. Space posts at least 4 hours apart. Spend the rest of your X time replying to mentor accounts.
- **Don't lean on threads until you near 10,000 followers.** Threads take hours to build and need an existing audience to push them. "It is very deflating to have a thread you spent hours on, then crickets." The closer to 10,000, the more a thread can pop.
- **Daily-thread experiment:** Post one thread per day, same content type, same time. Spend the rest of the day replying to mentor accounts and to anyone who replies to the thread. The algo notices: "this person is posting threads every day at the same time," and pushes it wider.
- **Threads have a season.** When he ran the daily thread in early 2025, by ~November the algo shifted away from threads and they stopped performing. Watch for the shift; don't assume any format is permanent.
- **Bookmarks are the prize metric.** Cited from Culture Explorer's algo discussion. Bookmarks signal real engagement and the algo rewards them heavily. Build posts people want to save or circle back to.
- **Per-post impressions inside a thread = read-through proof.** If every post in the thread holds high impressions, readers are going past the hook all the way to the end. That's the sign you want.
- **Cap the thread length.** He limits a list-thread to ~10 items max. Never go higher than 10.
- **Take emotion out of scheduling.** Never decide "this one's a banger, push it." You're always surprised which post hits. Write a batch, add to the queue, move on. Then double/triple/quadruple down on whatever actually performs — schedule 1–2 similar posts per day until that vein runs out of steam.
- **Never delete a poorly performing post.** Leave everything up. It may gain likes/reposts later, and new profile visitors scroll your history.
- **Keep the timeline clean.** Pin your highest-performing post. Limit reposts of other creators on your own profile so visitors see your work. Manually un-repost every 2–3 hours (and automate the same with Tweet Hunter).
- **Posting window (pre-algo-change method):** 6–8 posts/day. Used Tweet Hunter analytics to find when his audience was most active — roughly the first half of his day in his time zone. Posted more heavily in that early window. Set Tweet Hunter to repost each post every 4 hours, 3 times = a 12-hour visibility window.
- **Mentor accounts — build two lists:** (1) accounts in your niche, (2) accounts in adjacent niches you can add real context/commentary to (for him: music and movies). Replying in-niche gets you noticed; they see you reply often, see you're subscribed, and relationships/DMs/ghostwriting follow.
- **Every reply ends with a question.** Keeps the conversation going inside your own posts/threads. More conversation = more algo reward (Culture Explorer's "algo combo"). Keep the engagement in your ecosystem.
- **Reply to everyone you can.** Don't filter by verified status or profile photo. "If that person's interacting with my post and I have the time, I'm going to reply." Any notification, any interaction is good.
- **Pay to play — get Premium Plus, not Premium.** The biggest offering buys an algo boost and the narrative "this person is a Premium Plus customer." Hard for people to take you seriously without it. "You've got to pay the admission to get into the building, then go to work."
- **Cross-post discipline (Substack ↔ X):** Each platform gets its own flavor. Repurpose freely: X posts → Substack Notes; free Substack newsletters → X Articles; old daily threads → Substack articles (he has a system; 11 months of daily threads = an endless well).
- **Substack Notes by category (Orel/WriteStack method):** Don't post random Notes. List the viral Note types from Orel's presentation, write your own guardrails/rules, draft 10–12 examples yourself, then use AI for more. Only post Notes in those proven categories. Result: more restacks and likes over the following ~3 weeks. Worth the extra time once you're past the early stage.
- **Early-stage shortcut:** If you're new, just repost your X content straight to Substack Notes. Save the per-platform tailoring for later.
- **Time discipline beats perfection.** "Perfection doesn't always go viral." He's confident he can ship a full thread — hook, images, body, edits — in one hour if forced. Good enough to publish, then move on.

## Hook / thread / offer patterns

- **List-thread hook (two-panel visual):** name an aspirational thing, undercut it on price, promise cheaper alternatives that keep the feeling. His example:
  > "Bora Bora is beautiful enough to make almost anyone want to go. Then you check the prices. Here are 10 cheaper alternatives that still deliver the dream-island feeling."
  Two-panel graphic turned into a **video** (not a static image) so the post surfaces on X's mobile **video tab** for extra reach.
- **Body keeps people scrolling:** strong image + strong hook up top, then mix videos and images through the body to hold attention to the end.
- **Audience-participation post (run ~once every 2 weeks):** "Tell me a place and I'll share a photo/video of it." Feeds you a long reply chain, gives you future post ideas, lets you reply with media. Not viral on likes/reposts, but consistently high impressions because people read the whole chain. Play along with the joke answers (post a gif when someone names a random place).
- **Brand-adapted (KJV discernment / The Biblical Man):** the list-thread "aspiration → undercut → cheaper truth" maps to: "Everyone wants the megachurch comfort. Then you read the verse they skipped. Here are 10 hard sayings of Christ they edited out." Audience-participation maps to: "Name the verse that wrecked you. I'll show you what the KJV actually says." Every reply ends with a question, same rule.
- **Brand-adapted (Follow Me / scar-tissue):** "Tell me the moment you almost walked away from God. I'll reply with the night I almost did." Same participation-chain mechanic, confessional fuel.
- **Outreach / DM script (to a mentor account):** reply to their posts for a couple of weeks first, then DM straight:
  > "Hey, I'm so-and-so. You've probably seen me on the timeline replying to a lot of your stuff. Huge fan. Anything going on I can help with, or just want to chat? DMs are open."
  Don't over-engineer it. This led directly to a paid ghostwriting gig for one of his mentor accounts.

## Numbers and proof

- Reply goal for a small account: at least one high-performing reply a day with 50+ or 100+ likes.
- Yesterday's thread (Bora Bora): 39 reposts, 573 likes, ~945 bookmarks. Posted ~5:03 a.m. Central.
- Today's thread: 453,000 impressions at 7 hours in (posted ~4 a.m. Central), 586 bookmarks and climbing — on track to pass the prior thread's bookmark count. Pinned/highest post cited at 8 million impressions.
- Account daily impressions during the session climbed 453,000 → 460,000 → 953,540 (older threads/posts still "cooking"). His stretch target for the thread: 750,000 to 1,000,000.
- Each post inside today's thread held ~36,000+ impressions = full read-through.
- Thread length cap: 10 items.
- Post spacing for small accounts: 4+ hours apart, 3–4 posts/day.
- Repost automation: every 4 hours, 3 times = 12-hour window.
- Wrote daily threads for ~11 months → effectively unlimited Substack-article source material.
- Orel-format Notes: ~3 weeks of testing showed clear lift in restacks/likes.
- Mentor-list example account "Travel Axe" cited at ~43,000 followers (a smaller account worth replying to).

## Brand application (Adam's voice)

- Adam already lives the cadence: write daily, reply hard, let content pillars (KJV discernment, masculinity, scar-tissue) pick the topic — not a guess about which post is a "banger."
- Bookmarks are the metric to chase on The Biblical Man. Build saveable artifacts: verse breakdowns, "10 hard sayings" lists, discernment checklists people circle back to.
- Run the daily-thread experiment as a list-thread: aspiration the modern church sells, the undercut, the KJV truth. Turn yesterday's thread into today's Substack post — the 11-month well applies directly.
- Audience-participation chain fits Follow Me cold: ask readers for the wound, reply with yours. High impressions, deep relationship, endless confessional fuel.
- Every reply ends with a question. Keep the dialogue inside the post. Don't filter who you reply to.
- Premium Plus is non-negotiable for serious growth — pay to play.
- Substack Notes only in proven viral categories, not random. Draft your own first, AI for volume second.
