# The Builder Identity Worksheet

## Source
- File: The_Builder_Identity_Worksheet_1.txt
- Teacher: not named (Content Academy worksheet, Creation247 / Gumroad)
- Topic: builder identity worksheet (creator vs builder)

## Core thesis
Most people online are creators. The ones who win become builders. A builder names the world, the role, the content type, and the destination, then runs that identity for 90 days.

## Key frameworks / rules
- Creator vs builder split: a creator posts; a builder posts toward a named thing. Pick the builder side on purpose.
- Four-part identity stack (answer all four to define yourself):
  1. World you build in. The arena of ideas you live inside. Examples given: finance, culture, AI, faith, entrepreneurship, fitness, politics, technology.
  2. Role you play in that world. How people experience your voice. Examples given: teacher, commentator, storyteller, investigator, guide, curator, philosopher.
  3. Content type you will be known for. Examples given: essays, threads, articles, commentary, research, memes, interviews, tutorials.
  4. What you build toward. The destination. Examples given: newsletter, media brand, consulting, community, courses, events, full creator business.
- The 90-day proof test: finish the sentence "In 90 days, strangers will DM me assuming that I am building ____." If strangers can name what you are building, the identity is legible. If they cannot, it is not built yet.
- Direction over volume: the worksheet sets a single direction for the next 90 days, not a content quota. One world, one role, one content type, one destination.

## Hook/thread/offer patterns
- Identity-declaration hook (brand-adapted): state world plus role plus enemy in one line. Example: "I teach men to test every spirit by the King James Bible. Most are following voices they never weighed." World = faith/discernment. Role = teacher. Tension = the untested voice.
- The 90-day DM test as a content frame: write the post that makes a stranger DM you assuming what you are building. For Adam this is the question every post should pass before it ships.
- Offer ladder maps onto "what you build toward": free X and Notes presence, then the newsletter (The Biblical Man / Follow Me), then paid product / Vault as the destination. Each post should point one rung up that ladder.

## Numbers and proof
- 90 days: the horizon for building a legible identity.
- 4 questions define the builder identity (world, role, content type, destination).
