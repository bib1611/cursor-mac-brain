# Crush Writer's Block — The Daily Content Engine (Dino)

## Source
- File: `transcripts/How_to_Crush_Writers_Block.txt`
- Teacher: Dino (Art of Purpose), Creation 24/7 / Masterclass 24/7
- Topic: a day-in-the-life content blueprint that destroys writer's block — review yesterday, continue the conversation, or go find one
- Type: transcript (weekly writers workshop, recorded module)

## Core thesis
There is no blank page and no real writer's block. Content is a continual conversation with your timeline, not a batch you schedule on Sunday night. Every day you do one of exactly two things: further a conversation you already started, or go find one someone else started and run it through your own lens. X is not evergreen — a post gets algorithmic life for a day, maybe two or three at most — so you must feed the conversation daily.

## Key frameworks / rules

- **The two-ways law.** There are only two ways to create content. Either further a conversation you already had (add to it), or look at what someone else is doing and run it through your lens. The "what someone else is doing" half is just as important as the first.
- **Review yesterday first (5 minutes).** Before writing anything, spend about five minutes on how yesterday's tweets performed. That gives context for the day. Ask: do I need to continue a conversation, follow up, or change the angle on a tweet that did well? Start here and your day's tweets are far better tuned.
- **The scheduling mistake.** Loading a week of tweets into a scheduler on Sunday night kills engagement, because you are not having a conversation with your timeline — you give readers no way in. Schedulers are why "no one engages with my tweets."
- **The 24-hour scoring window.** Judge a post's numbers inside a 24-hour period, not at hour 11. A post still climbing gets another manual push (a repost) to extend it.
- **Define your own "good."** No spreadsheet required — read the natural likes and impressions for your account. Dino's personal threshold: anything over ~1,000 likes and ~20,000 impressions is a conversation that must be continued. But a 500-follower account getting 5 organic likes is incredible — set the bar to your own size.
- **Lean into the timely over the evergreen.** When two topics are both working, give first priority to the time-bound, top-of-mind one (a holiday, today's news) over the one you could post any day. Spend the perishable opportunity first.
- **The pitcher's arsenal.** Vary your post types like a pitcher varies pitches — fastball, curveball, slider, knuckleball. Throw only fastballs (all memes) and people tune out and never learn who you are. A versatile arsenal builds a dynamic brand. Not every pitch lands; expect some to bomb.
- **Research more than you write (75/25 to 80/20).** Spend more time consuming and curating than creating — Dino cites the culture critic at roughly 75/25 to 80/20 research-to-writing. Minimum ~30 minutes per day consuming. The smaller your account, the more this matters. Consuming wisely IS part of the creation process.
- **Kill two birds: replies = consumption.** Do your reply pass and your content mining in the same session. While leaving comments you scan the timeline for topics, news, and reusable material.
- **The find-a-conversation ladder (when your own timeline is dead).** Default the home feed to Following and scan chronologically first — it surfaces timely posts so your comments land early. If nothing sparks, go to the For You page (mostly garbage, but you mine the few good personal stories, testimonials, and memes). Bookmark anything reusable into the right folder.
- **The bookmark bank.** Keep an extensive, categorized bookmarks folder as a future-post engine: account-definition tweets, "based" tweets (cool ideas to read more about), great threads, great memes, community affiliate-sale posts, and auto-DM campaigns. Each is an inspiration for a future post.
- **Mine your own comment section.** Read your own replies every day. A standout comment under your post is a new meme or post waiting to be made, and continuing a thread inside your own comments is itself a content idea.
- **Mine your own thread internals.** For thread writers: look at your high-performing threads and the parts inside them. A line that popped inside a thread becomes a new standalone thread or a continued conversation.
- **Raid your past winners when blocked.** Out of ideas? Pull an old validated meme or post and refresh it — same format, new pictures or a sharper angle. Or shelve the dead topic and switch to another idea on your list.
- **Networking rooms as an idea source.** Use community "help branding" and "help tweets" rooms for tweet ideas and feedback. Help one person before you ask for help — you internalize the craft by teaching it and you build goodwill. (Adapt to whatever peer group you have; the principle is reciprocity, not the specific room.)
- **Train your audience to engage.** The ultimate goal: create conversation, then train your audience to keep engaging with your posts. Rewarding commenters conditions them to come back.

## Daily content blueprint (the bare minimums)
- **25 to 50 thoughtful replies per day**, targeted at the biggest accounts in your niche, fast/timely. Put those accounts on notification. (Note: the brain's PART 13 supersedes the raw number — run ~20 to 25 high-quality comments, never a soulless spray. Use Dino's intent, not the inflated count.)
- **One question post per day.**
- **One authentic / behind-the-scenes post per day** — what is going on in your life, a reflection, a confession.
- **One engagement post per day** — a meme, a listicle, or a headline.
- **One thread per week.** Content creation does not need to be hard: question post, authentic post, engagement post, consume daily, one thread a week.
- Post types are combinable — a question can live inside a meme, an engagement post can carry a reflection.

## Hook / pattern examples (brand-adapted)

- **The rewrite-a-winner move.** When a meme goes viral, the first move is to rewrite it — same idea, fresh picture or pushed more absurd. In the transcript, an Easter "old Europe vs. modern America" meme went viral after sitting dead ~6-8 hours; the play is to remake it and quote-tweet the poll. Brand version (Follow Me / Biblical Man): if a post on watered-down translations pops, rewrite it with a sharper image — "They handed you a Bible with the verses cut out. You called it a study edition."
- **Rewriting someone else's post through your lens.** Dino's live example: a "ladies, be feminine, be a mother" post becomes "men, be masculine. Be a father. Take your kids to church. Stop drinking every weekend. Spend time with your son." Same skeleton, your conviction. Brand version: a generic "read your Bible" post becomes "Read it in the King James. Then read what your modern version left out. Then ask who told you the originals were lost."
- **Reusing validated content as a comment.** Keep proven lines searchable and drop them as early comments when the timeline echoes the topic. Brand version under a post about lost manuscripts: "Your NIV deleted Acts 8:37. They told you the originals were lost. They lied."
- **Authentic-post engine.** The over-performing post in the transcript was personal — a confession about his wife (2,000 likes vs. an architecture post that bombed at ~200). The lesson: confessional and behind-the-scenes out-perform polished topical posts. This is the Follow Me / scar-tissue lane.

## Numbers and proof
- Algorithmic life of an X post: **one day, maybe two or three at most.**
- Easter meme poll: **95% of 11,000 votes** on one answer (surprised him).
- "Porn should not be allowed on X" post: **~400 likes** at 11 hours old; projected to ~650 likes / ~25,000 impressions after a manual repost.
- "Will the US ever elect a woman president" question post: **~32,000 impressions** (questions pull impressions, fewer likes).
- News-story post: **~10,000 impressions**, few likes.
- Personal post about his wife: **~2,000 likes** (a "great performer," over-performing).
- Architecture/Basilica post that bombed: **~200 likes**, despite the topic doing well before — caption or topic miss; not every post lands.
- Dino's continue-the-conversation threshold: **>1,000 likes and >20,000 impressions.**
- A 500-follower account: **5 organic likes is incredible** — scale the bar to your size.
- Research-to-write ratio: **roughly 75/25 to 80/20** (consume more than you create).
- Daily reply target in this lesson: **25 to 50** (use ~20 to 25 quality per PART 13).
- Time costs: ~5 min reviewing yesterday; ~30 min/day consuming and curating.
- All numbers cited were **100% organic** — no engagement groups.

## Brand application (Adam's voice)
- Wake, review yesterday's numbers, ask one question: which conversation do I continue today. Biblical Man (KJV discernment, masculinity) usually carries the question and engagement posts; Follow Me carries the authentic/confessional post.
- The authentic post is the one that over-performs — lean into scar tissue, not theory. Write from wounds.
- Lead with the perishable: a feast day, a denominational headline, a translation controversy in the news beats the evergreen explainer the same day.
- Keep a one-line idea list and a searchable archive of old winners. Block = raid your own vault, refresh a proven line, or switch topics. Never stare at a blank page.
- Vary the arsenal: discernment meme, masculinity headline, a question, a confession. All fastballs (all rage-memes) and readers stop knowing who you are.
- Mine the confessional archive and your own comment sections as the topic engine — your readers' pain is the prompt.
- Voice-to-text the replies to move fast, then tighten by hand.
