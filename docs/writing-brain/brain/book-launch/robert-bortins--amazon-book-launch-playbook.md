# Amazon Book Launch Playbook

## Source
- File: Masterclass26 How To Launch on Amazon (1).txt
- Teacher: Robert B. (X.com/TheRobertBShow), "2026 Amazon Book Launch Playbook"
- Topic: Step-by-step Amazon/KDP book launch built on a real launch that hit #78 in New Releases, refined with former Amazon employees.

## Core thesis
Amazon is pay-to-play, not build-it-and-they-come. Wins come from a virtuous cycle: visibility drives sales, sales improve rankings, rankings increase organic visibility. You win that cycle by positioning into winnable categories, listing early to bank preorder momentum and reviews, and spending aggressively on search ads.

## Key frameworks / rules
- Four-phase timeline:
  - 90 days out (Foundation): KDP publisher setup, Brand Registry (unlocks A+ Content, brand analytics), category research, Author Central page.
  - 60 days out (Review Building): ARCs out, ad campaigns in test mode, additional formats built, supplementary products prepped.
  - 30 days out (Acceleration): optimize the product page, ramp ad spend, set up FBA / Prime, build the email sequence.
  - Launch week (Maximum Momentum): multi-channel push, max ad spend, hourly ranking monitoring, social proof harvesting.
  - Post-launch (Sustaining): keep ads on, request reviews systematically, expand the catalog, analyze and iterate.
- Category rule: identify 3-5 categories where you can realistically reach Top 3. Choose by winnability, not prestige. Being #1 in a small category beats #500 in a large one.
- Review threshold rule: get 10+ reviews before launch day. Ten reviews is the first critical social-proof threshold. Never incentivize positive reviews; always ask for honest feedback.
- List early rule: have the preorder page live 60+ days. Waiting until launch day forfeits preorder momentum and review-gathering time.
- Top 3 rule: only the top 3 search results get meaningful clicks. Bid aggressively enough to hold Top 3, or the spend is wasted.
- Prime rule: Prime eligibility is non-negotiable. Most customers filter for Prime-only. Run FBA, never stock out (stockouts kill momentum).
- Backend keyword rule: optimize all 7 backend keywords using Helium 10 data.
- Format ladder (multiple formats = more revenue, each plays a role):
  - Kindle $9.99-$14.99 = discovery and volume play.
  - Paperback $19.99-$24.99 = authority piece.
  - Hardcover $26.99-$32.99 = premium gift positioning.
  - Audiobook via ACX for Audible.
  - Discussion Guide $7.99-$12.99 = supplementary product.
- Kindle bonus rule: add exclusive content to Kindle (bonus chapter, extended foreword) so buyers cross-purchase the physical book.
- Ad spend ladder (% of projected revenue): 10-15% in test/foundation, 15-20% during launch week, then settle back to a sustainable 10% post-launch. Monitor hourly during launch week, not daily.
- Metrics to track: Sales Rank (hourly, immediate momentum), Best Sellers Rank (stable, recent velocity), Category Rankings (aim #1 in multiple), New Release Rankings (best shot at a badge).
- Review-button rule: hit Amazon's "Request a Review" button on every order. Build toward 50, 100, 500 review milestones.

## Email launch sequence (5 sends)
1. Pre-launch announcement (30 days out)
2. Behind-the-scenes content (20 days out)
3. Bonus content reveal (10 days out)
4. Launch day announcement with urgency
5. Review request (7 days post-launch)

## Ad targeting patterns
- Sponsored Products targeting competitor titles by name (the source targeted Pete Hegseth's book).
- Keyword campaigns bidding on high-intent search terms.
- Category targeting in your chosen categories.
- Launch week: target multiple competitor titles simultaneously, add new keyword variations as you find them, pause underperformers, double down on positive-ROI campaigns.

## Numbers and proof
- Source launch reached #78 in New Releases ("Woke and Weaponized").
- 40% of book sales come from Amazon search ads (primary discovery mechanism).
- 10%+ of Kindle buyers will buy the physical copy if given a reason.
- 10+ reviews = first critical threshold before launch day.
- Ad spend: 10-15% test, 15-20% launch week, 10% sustained.
- Top 3 search results capture the meaningful clicks.
- A $50/month ad budget will not move the needle (underspending is a listed mistake).

## Common mistakes to avoid
- Listing only on launch day (lose preorder momentum and review runway).
- Picking categories for prestige over winnability.
- Underspending on ads (Amazon is pay-to-play).
- Ignoring Prime eligibility.
- Stopping promotion after launch week.
- Not tracking metrics obsessively.
- Forgetting reviews are social proof; ten is the first threshold.

## Brand application (Adam's voice)
- The book is the discovery vehicle. Kindle priced low for reach, then the Vault, study guides, and physical copies catch the margin. Same offer ladder Adam already runs on Substack and deadhidden.org, just on Amazon rails.
- ARC list = Adam's most engaged paid subs and the confessional readers. Ask for honest feedback, never bribe a star. KJV crowd smells a paid review.
- Categories: pick narrow and winnable. "Christian Men's Issues," "Christian Discernment," not "Religion & Spirituality" where you drown at #500. Being #1 in a small room beats being unseen in a big one.
- Email sequence maps to his existing cadence: tease the wound, show the work, drop the bonus, launch with urgency, ask for the review. Five sends, no filler.
- Supplementary product = a study guide or discussion guide priced $7.99-$12.99. He already builds these. List them as a companion so Amazon keeps the customer on platform.
- Brutal flag: this is infrastructure-heavy. A launch only matters if there is a finished book. Do not build the Amazon machine before the manuscript bleeds onto the page.
