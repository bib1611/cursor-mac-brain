# Culture Critic — Six Figures Writing Online

## Source
- File: `CultureCritc.txt`
- Teacher: Culture Critic (X account, ~1.5M soon 1.6M followers, 100,000 email subscribers), interviewed by AOP / "Dino" (Dino Anthony) for the Masterclass community.
- Topic: How to make six figures writing online starting from zero — X growth, email list conversion, paid Substack, offer ladder.

## Core thesis
You build nothing on X itself; the algorithm can change tomorrow. The real asset is the email list you pull off X. Build authority by giving away free, high-effort, deliberately-viral content, convert the most engaged readers into a low-ticket paid list, then ladder them up to higher-ticket offers through one-to-one personal contact.

## Key frameworks / rules

- **Build alongside X, never on it.** Any algorithm-based platform is rented ground. Every day, post a CTA link buried deep in a thread to pull your most engaged readers into the email list. Do not stop making X content to "go build email" — that decay kills you, and external links got muted by the algorithm anyway. The list is built by making X bigger, not by extracting from it.
- **Be scientific, not a thought-dump.** Most people dump whatever they're thinking. Instead, know your baselines: baseline likes, impressions, retweets, comments (comments now matter a lot). Make more of what beats baseline, drop what doesn't. Doing the same dud repeatedly and expecting a different result is the definition of insanity.
- **Only write what can go viral.** Don't spend 5-8 hours researching a thread with no viral ceiling. Every post on the timeline should have at least mainstream-interest potential. Deliberately take angles on proven popular tropes.
- **Make a big topic out of a niche topic.** Position so a maximally broad audience cares. Not "Vespasian rebuilt this building" (niche), but "ancient Rome had a technology no one knows about" (broad). You don't have to love ancient Rome to click it.
- **The 5x research rule.** Spend roughly five times more time researching than writing. Mine X search, past X posts, old Reddit threads and their top comments, Google, historical content that already went viral on the topic. Find the images that work and the hooks that work. Don't copy flat out — give your own spin.
- **Run a permanent idea swipe-file.** Keep one very long Word doc or Apple Notes file, added to for 18+ months. Drop every stray thought or article take into it. Each morning, scroll the list, pick the topic with the broadest-care potential, and only select it once a hook strikes you. Never run out of content.
- **Hook first, thread second.** Come up with the hook before you know what the thread says. The Rome/America thread started as "write something uplifting about America as a mini-Rome" with no hook; days later he saw an image of a Roman barracks grid plan that paralleled New York's grid, and the hook fell out of that.
- **Self-made images, never stock.** A tailor-made image is the strongest signal that the thread is high-effort and worth a click/follow. "Stock photos, stock personality." Basic image editing (Canva, ~5 minutes) is one of the highest-ROI moves on an early-stage platform — be early the way Mr. Beast was early on YouTube thumbnails.
- **Easy-to-write posts are often the best.** The best posts frequently write themselves fast (the Rome thread, ~2 hours; a 2.5M-view meme, ~2 minutes). If a topic is good it nearly writes itself.
- **Writer's block protocol.** If stuck, shelve the topic entirely and pick another. Or go to your own back-catalog: find a past thread that performed, read its retweets/comments, take a fresh angle on the same proven topic.
- **Your two jobs on X:** (1) Build authority by giving away free, valuable, interesting content. (2) Convert that into one-to-one relationships with real people. Authority is worthless without the personal connection.
- **Two kinds of value — give one away, guard the other.** "Entertainment value" (interesting, dopamine, builds relationship — e.g. how Rome and America parallel) you can give away endlessly. "Utility value" (the actual tool/system someone could turn into $10K) you guard, especially if you're a coach. If you give the whole system away, no one hires you. If you're pure content (no coaching), you can give infinitely and just sell more/exclusive content.
- **Build a long ladder, weight it at the bottom.** Don't chase only the big $10K high-ticket buyer. A $2-$4 sub makes near-zero money but converts a free reader into a customer — and a paying customer (even $4) is far more likely to buy again than a free timeline follower. It is always cheaper to keep a customer than acquire one. Postpone the reward, ladder up.
- **Plug multiple times.** In a space, never plug the offer only at the end. Pin it in the jumbotron/nest and plug it at the half-hour and top of the hour. People have ADHD; one plug isn't enough.
- **Never turn down a call.** Send a personal thank-you note to every paid subscriber, invite them to reach out, and never say no to a phone call. This surfaces the warm buyers for any future high-ticket offer.
- **Sell on an established platform.** Choose the platform with the longer track record and better revenue (Substack over a newer tool like beehiiv) for peace of mind — new platforms fold and strand your readers. You still own the email list on Substack.
- **Persuasive writing is the load-bearing skill.** If you cannot write, you will struggle to grow no matter the tactics. Going from good to great with a coach is attainable; going from zero is brutal. Writing persuasively (and speaking) is one of the highest-ROI skills that exists — everything else here sits on top of it.

## Hook / thread / offer patterns

- **Three-part hook (cliffhanger):**
  1. Set the stage with a retweetable, widely-cared-about premise.
  2. A "cheesy" bridge / open loop ("It all started when...").
  3. A twist that subverts the conventional explanation.
  - Example used: "Past societies produced so much beauty because they knew that math and beauty were deeply connected. It all started when Pythagoras discovered something mind-blowing about reality. The universe is not made of matter, but music." (2.2M views.)
- **The "you-think-you-know, here's-the-twist" hook:** thing you care about + conventional explanation you assume + why it's actually wrong. Example: "America was founded to be the true successor of ancient Rome — but you don't know how deep the parallels run." (39M views, boosted by big-account retweets.)
- **Buried CTA pattern:** put the link at the very bottom of the thread, then retweet (or pin) the thread so the link sits at the top of your profile for anyone who visits after reading.
  - CTA copy used: "If you enjoy threads like this, I go deeper in my free newsletter. Do not miss tomorrow's email." (Urgency + active-newsletter framing.)
- **Demonstrate-don't-claim:** you can signal value either by teaching it or by visibly doing it. A thread that performs (e.g. 500K views) proves you can sell the product without giving away the method.
- **Lead magnet, done right:** not a 100-page free ebook with no CTA. A short 5-page PDF "quick win" + a ~10-minute companion video where they hear your voice and you invite DMs, with hard, explicit calls to action throughout. People value time and a quick win, and a free gift earns you the right to sell hard.
- **Spaces as the conversion engine:** host a weekly space on the week's topic; it surfaces your most engaged readers, generates content (transcribe the space into an email/threads), and lets you pitch directly. Tell people to "stop what you're doing right now" and sign up.
- **High-ticket release:** don't email the full list. Email only the few thousand who already pay you low-ticket, and offer them coaching / one-to-one / courses.

### KJV-discernment brand adaptation
- Three-part hook: "The church has forgotten what fear of God means. It started when one verse got mistranslated out of every modern Bible. The God of the KJV is not the God they preach on Sunday." (Premise + open loop + twist.)
- "You-think-you-know" hook for masculinity/scar-tissue: "Every man wants to be the head of his house. But you don't know how far you've already surrendered." Then the twist.
- Big-topic-from-niche: not "a study of 1 Timothy 2," but "The Bible commands one thing about manhood that no pastor will say out loud."

## Brand application (Adam's voice)
- The list is the asset. X is rented. Pull readers off X into Substack and email every day. Adam already does this; the discipline is the buried daily CTA, not a one-time pin.
- Know the baselines. Adam should hold a running sense of his baseline impressions, likes, and comments, and write toward what beats them — confessional scar-tissue posts and hard KJV-discernment takes, dropping what dies.
- Hook first, then bleed. Write the one-line hook before the post. If it won't make a man stop scrolling, don't spend the morning on it.
- Quick-win lead magnet, not a bloated free ebook. A short PDF plus a voice/video where they hear Adam, with a hard CTA into the paid Vault.
- Long ladder, weighted low. A $2-$4 entry converts a free reader into a buyer who is far likelier to buy the Vault later. Keep existing customers; that is where the six figures already live.
- Two kinds of value: give away the wound and the conviction (entertainment/relationship value) freely; guard the full system/implementation if it's a coaching or product offer.
