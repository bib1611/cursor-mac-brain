# Auto-DM Lead Magnet Capture SOP

## Source
- File: `Tweet_Hunter_Auto_DMs.txt`
- Teacher: not named (Masterclass 24/7 archive instructor)
- Topic: capturing emails at scale by pairing an X auto-DM tweet with a free lead magnet, using Tweet Hunter

## Core thesis
A single tweet wired to an auto-DM turns likes and replies into email subscribers on autopilot. The reader gets a free guide, you get their email, and the tweet keeps working while you do nothing. This is the highest-leverage way to grow an email list from X.

## Key frameworks / rules
- The capture loop: tweet promises a free guide -> reader likes or replies -> auto-DM fires with the link -> reader opts in on the landing/Gumroad page -> you get the email, they get the magnet.
- Trigger on the easiest action. Set the auto-DM to fire on like OR reply (either condition). Tell people to "like and reply" but accept either. Make the action as simple as possible so more people qualify.
- DM must mirror the tweet. The auto-DM benefits must be in line with what the tweet promised. The reader should know exactly what they are getting. Close alignment beats none, but match it.
- Add urgency to the DM. "Link valid for 24 hours" pushes people to actually click and download instead of saving it for later.
- Earn the right to run it first. Only run auto-DM campaigns once you have an established page: a blue check, two-factor ID set up, and a history X recognizes. Do not run these from a new or unverified account, or X may flag the sends.
- Cap sends at 500. That is Tweet Hunter's recommended ceiling. Hit 500 and the DM sends get throttled/shut down for that run.
- After the cap, add a manual call to action. Once auto-DMs shut off at 500, drop a CTA reply yourself so latecomers still get the link.
- Automate fully. Tweet Hunter does the sending. You write the tweet, set advanced options, turn on auto-DM, load the reply message, and hit tweet. No manual DMing.
- Make the magnet free with a name and price. Hosting the guide as a free product on Gumroad (named, priced at free) still produces sales from the same page, plus the email capture.
- Verify the preview before sending. Tweet Hunter shows both the tweet as it looks on a phone and the exact DM the reader will receive. Check both, then publish.
- Sends start in about 3 to 4 minutes after the tweet goes live.

## Hook / thread / offer patterns
- Transformation-receipt hook (the pinned-tweet pattern):
  "Three years ago: 5,000 followers. Zero dollars in online sales. Zero engagement. Today: [X, Y, Z]. I quantified the system and built a free course that teaches you the same. Like and comment and it's yours."
- Future/benefit-stack hook:
  "X is the future of social media. I wrote a free guide to help you on your journey. It shows you everything: how to create a profile, how to gain 10,000 followers, how to sell offers on X. To get it, follow me and like and reply."
- Auto-DM reply template:
  "Thank you for replying. Here is your free guide: [link]. You will learn how to create a profile, how to gain 10,000 followers, and how to profit by selling offers. Link valid for 24 hours."
- KJV-discernment brand adaptation (Biblical Man):
  - Tweet: "Most men read the Bible and stay blind. I built a free guide on how to read for discernment, not just information. Like and reply and it is yours."
  - Auto-DM: "Thank you for replying. Here is your free guide: [link]. You will learn how to test a teaching, how to spot a counterfeit gospel, and how to read the KJV for conviction. Link valid for 24 hours."
- Follow Me (scar-tissue) brand adaptation:
  - Tweet: "I almost lost my faith and my family in the same year. I wrote down everything that pulled me back. Free. Like and reply and I will send it."
  - Auto-DM: "Thank you for replying. Here is the guide: [link]. The exact steps I used to rebuild. Link valid for 24 hours."
