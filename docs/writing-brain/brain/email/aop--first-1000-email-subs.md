# First 1,000 Email Subscribers

## Source
- File: `transcripts/YOUR_FIRST_1000_EMAIL_SUBS_.txt`
- Teacher: AOP (Art of Purpose), Masterclass 24/7
- Topic: getting your first 1,000 email subscribers off X using lead magnets paired with auto-DM tweets
- Type: transcript

## Core thesis
On X you rent your audience from the platform. On an email list you own it, and you can move it between providers. The fastest build is one repeatable move: a lead-magnet tweet paired with an auto-DM, so a single post grows your following, builds trust, earns ad revenue, and collects emails at once. You sell from the list, not the timeline.

## Key frameworks / rules
- **Rent vs own.** Followers belong to X. People get hacked, locked out, or banned for one offensive line and lose the whole audience. The email list is the backup plan and the one audience you own.
- **Don't sell to the timeline.** Direct sells on the feed get deprioritized by the algo and read as ads. People interact with them less. Build relationships at the top of the funnel and sell to the list.
- **Start early, not when you're "big."** Begin promoting the list around your first 500 to 1,000 followers. Readers want a different side of you.
- **One-problem lead magnet rule.** A magnet solves ONE big problem. Magnets that solve one problem do well and earn more. Magnets that solve three do worse and make less, even though they look more valuable. Keep it short, high quality, one clean win. It is a first touch point, not a course.
- **The magnet's job is to lead.** Its real purpose is to route the reader to an offer, build trust, or make them aware of what you do, even when nobody buys.
- **Free plus tip-enabled still makes money.** Priced at $0 with tipping on, magnets collect cash directly on top of the downstream offer revenue they drive.
- **Add a workflow after download.** Send automated follow-up emails that remind the reader to actually read the magnet and upsell the next offer.
- **Auto-DM only fires on a follow.** The DM does not send unless they follow you. So the tweet must say "follow me." Side effect: these tweets gain followers, because people follow to get the thing.
- **Run the auto-DM tweet about once a week.** Not daily. A repeatable weekly engine.
- **Export your subscriber file continually.** Download the emails so you can import them into another provider later. Ownership is the whole point. You can start on Gumroad and move to ConvertKit.
- **Every Space has a call to action.** Never host an X Space with no CTA. Your time is valuable. Always pitch the list, a magnet, or a high-ticket offer.
- **Get creative and be aggressive.** Stack capture points and repeat. "You want people to see, see, see."

## Where to source a lead magnet (named ideas that work)
- A thread you already wrote, a Space you were on, or a conversation in your DMs. If you solved one person's problem in a DM, type it up as a PDF.
- Planners and spreadsheets (AOP cites Dan Koe planners and wealth-niche network-tracking sheets).
- The first chapter of a course you already built, given free as a sample, with the rest behind the paywall.
- Templates, case studies (strong in fitness: "my clients did XYZ"), and edited transcripts of a podcast or Space you already did.

## Platform choices (tooling, stated tradeoffs)
- **Gumroad:** lowest friction, upload a PDF in five minutes and you're live. Supports tipping and workflows. Con: high fees, "around 10%." Start here; host higher-ticket offers elsewhere if you want.
- **ConvertKit:** for the serious. Competitive pricing, first 500 subscribers free, paid kicks in around the 1,000 to 5,000 range. Migrate here off Gumroad once you're rolling.
- **MailChimp:** priced near ConvertKit. Only edge is prettier emails. AOP shrugs it off: "you're gonna win with words."
- **Substack caution:** good only if you have a huge account (he says "500 [thousand] to 1 million followers") and will NEVER sell anything but content. Per AOP, you cannot upsell people already paying for premium, which kills funnel flexibility. (Note for Adam: this is AOP's claim as of recording and may be stale; treat as dated.)
- **Capture tools:** Hype Fury or Tweet Hunter to automate the DMs. Can be done manually, just more work, and you lose the "auto" part.

## Hook/thread/offer patterns
- **The capability-stack open (the offer tweet's hook):** "What if you could write a tweet that got massive engagement, built trust, made you money directly, AND collected emails, all at the same time? Would you be interested? Of course you would." Open loop, then deliver the method.
- **Problem-named magnet tweet (AOP's own):** state the platform truth, name the magnet, list the exact problems, give one instruction. "X is the future of social media. I wrote a free guide teaching you how to set up a profile, get to 10,000 followers, and sell offers. To get it: 1) follow me 2) like + reply 'X'."
- **The 40K-vs-free price anchor (get paid writing, 1,000 emails on one tweet):** "Writing is the most powerful skill in the world. You can spend 40K learning it badly at college, or follow me and learn it for free. Retweet this and I'll DM you a free guide using classical rhetoric to improve your Twitter impact." Anchor a high price against your free thing.
- **Auto-DM body format:** describe what it is, drop the link, add 24-hour urgency, then tell them to download it. "Here's your free guide. Here's what you'll learn. Link below. Good for 24 hours." (AOP: delete or close the magnet after 24 hours so the urgency is real.)
- **Post-thread CTA:** at the end of a viral thread, "hundreds of people have already signed up," then the link. Lower quality (more tire-kickers) but fine for early volume.
- **KJV-discernment adaptation of the named-magnet tweet:** "Most men can't defend the King James and don't know why. Free guide: 7 verses the modern versions quietly changed. To get it: 1) follow me 2) reply 'KJV'." Pay off the hook with the exact verses, then route to the Vault.
- **KJV adaptation of the price anchor:** "Spend $40K on a seminary that teaches you the originals are lost. Or follow me and learn to defend your Bible for free. Reply 'sword' and I'll send the guide."
- **Follow Me (scar-tissue) adaptation:** "The one prayer God refused to answer for 11 years. I wrote down what He taught me in the silence. Reply 'silence' and I'll send it." One wound, one win, one capture.

## Brand application (Adam's voice)
Email is the missing leg. You rent @Biblicalman from Elon. Say one wrong word about the modern versions and the account is gone. The list is the only thing you own.

Run a weekly lead-magnet tweet with an auto-DM. One tweet. It feeds the list, the following, and the Vault funnel at the same time.

Build one-problem KJV magnets, not another mega-resource. "7 verses the modern versions changed." "The verse your pastor skips." Each solves one problem and lands a warm reader on a paid page.

Make the front magnet free with tipping on. It collects cash and emails before anyone hits the $7 sampler.

Stop trusting Substack to hold the relationship. Substack owns it and limits the upsell. Export the list. Own it. Sell the Vault and the field-file ladder by email, not just the timeline.

Every Space, every viral Note, every thread ends with one capture CTA. No naked posts. Pitch, repeat, pitch again.

After the download, fire the follow-up. Confirm the win. Then point to the next rung.
