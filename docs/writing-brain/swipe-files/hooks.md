# HOOK AND THREAD SWIPE FILE

Pull from this when writing. Two layers: the brand-native faith hooks (use first), then the proven generic frameworks adapted to the discernment niche. Every hook must pass the honesty gate: the post pays off what the hook promised, or the reader never forgives it.

---

## LAYER 1 — THE FIVE BRAND-NATIVE HOOKS (faith, masculinity, discernment)

### 1. The Paradigm Flip. "It's not X, it's Y."
Validate the symptom, re-diagnose the root.
- "It's not church hurt. It's conviction you didn't like."
- "It's not a quiet season in your faith. It's a dead pulpit preaching from a dead translation."
- "Your family isn't under attack. Your household is just unled."

### 2. The Objective Authority. "[Category] fact: ..."
Bypasses the critical filter. Use "Doctrinal fact:", "Dispensational fact:", "Scriptural fact:".
- "Dispensational fact: The Sermon on the Mount wasn't written to you. Stop trying to steal Israel's mail."
- "Scriptural fact: The Antichrist won't show up looking like a monster. He'll show up looking like your favorite megachurch pastor."

### 3. The Mirror Test / Brutal Call-Out.
Name a specific observable behavior, then attack the premise. The most-shared mechanic. Specificity wins.
- "You tithe to a building. You won't pray with your wife."
- "You've got opinions about end times. You can't name the last book you finished in the Book."

### 4. The Velvet Rope. Identity qualifier.
Define who is allowed to read it. Tribal belonging plus defense.
- "Man to man. When was the last time you opened the Book before you opened your screen?"
- "To the men letting their wives lead the theology of the house. You have abandoned your post."

### 5. The Action-Talk Gap. Hypocrisy exposer.
Passive consumption versus active discipline.
- "You complain about the culture. You complain about the government. But your Bible has dust on it."
- "You say you believe the Book. But you submit to a pastor who tells you the originals are lost."

## LAYER 2 — SIX PROVEN HOOK FRAMEWORKS (from the TTT "0 to 200k" cheat sheet)

Adapt the pattern, keep the voice. Each one creates an open loop the reader needs to close.

### Contrarian Hook
Pattern: "{Common belief} is wrong. Here's what {group who actually wins} does."
Why: pattern interruption forces attention.
Brand version: "Daily quiet time is killing your walk. Here's what men who actually grow do instead."

### Secret Observation Hook
Pattern: "I discovered {surprising insight} after {specific study}."
Why: authority plus curiosity.
Brand version: "I read Revelation 40 times before I noticed the one word every pastor skips."

### Hidden Reality Hook
Pattern: "This {ordinary thing} is actually {surprising contrast}, and almost nobody talks about it."
Why: cognitive dissonance.
Brand version: "The most-quoted verse at weddings is a courtroom curse. Nobody reads the context."

### Extreme Rarity Hook
Pattern: "This is {extreme example}, one of the {superlative} on Earth, and here's why it matters."
Why: humans fixate on rarity and extremes.
Brand version: "There is one prayer in Scripture God refused to answer. It should terrify you."

### Mistake Hook (loss aversion)
Pattern: "How {they} lost {result} by {common mistake}."
Why: people move faster to avoid pain than to chase pleasure.
Brand version: "How a good father raised three kids who left the faith. He did everything the church told him."

### Process Hook
Pattern: "I {specific action} and got {specific result} in {timeframe}. Here's how."
Why: specific outcome plus mysterious method.
Brand version: "I read the Bible 20 minutes a day for a year. It wrecked my marriage in the best way. Here's how."

## WINNING-HOOK INGREDIENTS (check the line has at least two)
curiosity, specificity, emotion, tension, contrast, numbers, strong visual.
Vague writing feels generic. Specific writing feels believable. "Rome has history" loses. "Rome is one of the few cities where 2,000-year-old ruins sit beside everyday life" wins. Same rule for Scripture: name the chapter, the number, the exact word.

## THREAD ARCHITECTURE
- Hook (diagnosis, lines 1 to 2). Twist (staccato symptoms, lines 3 to 5). Bridge ("Here are 5 brutal but biblical steps. Save this. 1/8"). Body (numbered, scannable).
- 7 to 10 parts. Shorter feels thin, longer drops off.
- The hook matters more than the body. If nobody stops scrolling, nothing else matters. Spend more time on the hook than the body.
- First line of a thread becomes the Substack article title. Second line becomes the subtitle.

## REPLY STRATEGY (the early growth engine)
- 25 to 50 replies a day on larger in-niche accounts that already have distribution.
- A great reply stands alone, adds a new angle, creates emotion, reads fast, leans slightly contrarian. It feels like a post people wish they had written.
- Weak: "Amen." Strong: a one-line truth that makes them click your profile.
- Speed beats perfection. Be early. Keep folders of images, memes, and clips ready to reply instantly.

## THE ESCALATION PRINCIPLE (repurposing is leverage)
One strong idea is never used once. Rewrite it at least 6 ways: post, thread, article, newsletter, video, carousel, reply, Substack note. Same idea performs differently with different wording, visual, timing, format, or emotional framing. Never assume a good idea failed after one attempt.

## RESEARCH FRAMEWORK (most viral ideas already exist in fragments)
Mine Reddit, YouTube, comment sections, X bookmarks, Grok, Perplexity. Look for emotional reactions, recurring frustrations, controversy, hidden gems, highly shared posts. Comments are often more valuable than the original post. Organize, refine, simplify, and present through the brand's perspective and voice.

## DISTRIBUTION > CREATION
Publishing is the beginning, not the end. After posting: reply to comments, engage immediately, leverage communities, reshare evergreen winners, keep content circulating. Most creators post and disappear. Distribution is the job now.

## LAYER 3 — NET-NEW HOOKS FROM THE 2026 MASTERCLASS DISTILL BATCH
- Open-with-a-number: 'After 17 years teaching Sunday school, here is every hard truth about raising sons in the faith I could come up with.' (number = credibility + input bias + promised value dump, best item first)
- The three-part cliffhanger: scene line + curiosity loop + twist. KJV: 'Past churches produced men who feared God. It started when one translation got quietly swapped out. What your pastor reads from is not the book your grandfather was saved by.'
- Contrast-with-two-named-images (drives quotes): 'At the megachurch, they ran the fog machine. At the old country church, they read the King James out loud.'
- Named-conduit (name the brand, then the blade): 'The NIV has sold 450 million copies. Every one softened a verse God meant to cut. You deserve the blade He wrote.'
- Big-topic-from-niche (broaden the want): 'They deleted a verse about the Trinity from your Bible and nobody noticed.' (instead of 'the textual variant in 1 John 5:7')
- Reply-chain audience prompt ('name it, I'll show it', runs ~every two weeks): 'Drop the one sin you can't kill. I'll give you the KJV verse that names it.'
- Price-anchor lead-magnet capture: 'Spend 40K on a seminary that says the originals are lost, or follow me and learn to defend your Bible free. Reply SWORD and I'll DM you the guide.'
- Proof-and-promise auto-DM tweet: 'Ten years ago I taught Sunday school and could not defend one verse under fire. I wrote a free guide: the verses your modern translation gutted, and how to answer for them. Like and reply SWORD and it is yours.'
- The talking-to-myself post: 'Didn't want to open the Book this morning. Tired. Numb. Opened it anyway. Psalm 23 was waiting. It always is.'
- Rewrite-the-adjacent-niche-post: take a 'wives, submit' post and flip it to 'men, lead the theology of your house. Open the Book before you open your phone. Pray with your wife tonight. Stop outsourcing your sons to a youth pastor you have never read.'
- Hot-take-then-elaborate (state the polarizing line standalone, stop, elaborate below): 'Don't marry out of love. Marry out of strategy.'
- Subtitle-as-second-person-command (doubles as a confrontation hook): 'Reclaim the head of your house and stop apologizing for the Word.'

## LAYER 4 — REPLY OPENERS AND CAPTURE HOOKS (P1 2026 BATCH)
- Add-to-it reply opener: 'Good post, man. Here is what I would add.' then your own angle. Never bare 'Amen.'
- Story-graft reply: lay your own lived one-liner over the creator's frame (his 'I retired my wife 23 years ago' move). Brand: 'I taught Sunday school 17 years before I could defend one verse under fire.'
- Reuse-an-old-post reply: drop your validated standalone line when a timeline post echoes it. KJV: 'Your NIV deleted Acts 8:37. They told you the originals were lost. They lied.'
- Disagreeable-bait highlight: post a likable line as bait, then pull the sharpest objector up and challenge it. 'Make it make sense.'
- Name-it-I-show-it reply prompt: 'Drop the verse your pastor skipped. I will tell you why he skipped it.'
- Humble-flex study shape: hard thing happened, here is what I learned. The brag earns attention, the lesson earns the share.
- Sample-then-gate KJV capture: 'Here is the one verse your modern translation gutted, side by side. Want all 17, free? See the next tweet.'
- Before-and-after faith capture: 'This man could not pray with his wife a year ago. No program. No guilt. Just three things. Want the exact three? See the next tweet.'
- Launch explainer post: 'Here is what The Biblical Man is. How often I send. What you get free. What opens up when you go paid.'
- Migration warm-up email: 'We are moving the list. One of the last emails from this address. Going forward it comes from Dead Hidden on Substack. Same fight, sharper sword.'
- Recommendation sentence (always write the line, never bare): 'Subscribe to him. He will make you a harder-to-fool man.'
- Founding-tier pitch: 'Founding members get the full archive, every paid drop, and the key to the Vault. One door, everything behind it.'
- Inbound DM opener (faith-adapted): 'Thanks for reaching out. Tell me where you are with the Book right now and where you want to be.'
- Why-now internal-urgency line: 'Every day you do not open the Book, your house drifts one more day without a head.'
