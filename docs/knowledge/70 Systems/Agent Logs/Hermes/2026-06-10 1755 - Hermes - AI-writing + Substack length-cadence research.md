## PART 1 — AI WRITING / DETECTION

### Verdict on detectors
- "Passing detectors" is not a winnable or coherent goal. Detectors score perplexity + burstiness.
- Declaration of Independence scored 98.5% AI (decrypt.co). Genesis and the US Constitution flagged. KJV-saturated formal prose WILL false-positive no matter what Adam does.
- Stanford (Zou et al.): 61.3% avg false-positive rate on human TOEFL essays; 97% flagged by at least one detector.
- Vanderbilt, Yale, Johns Hopkins disabled Turnitin AI detection over false accusations.
- The real jury: readers and rival writers. Heavy AI users now spot AI text at ~90% accuracy (Carnegie Mellon via vrid.ai).

### The 2026 tell list (evolution: 2024 = words, 2026 = structure/rhythm)
Reference: Wikipedia "Signs of AI writing" + Decrypt + Pangram + Olivia Cal.
1. Negative parallelism ("It's not X, it's Y") — now the #1 tell. THIS IS ADAM'S SIGNATURE MOVE. Ration to one per piece.
2. Tier-1 vocab: delve, tapestry, testament, pivotal, landscape, foster, robust, "serves as/stands as."
3. Rule of three (tricolons) everywhere.
4. Mathematically even rhythm — uniform sentence/paragraph lengths, frictionless transitions.
5. Em dash DENSITY (not presence — human NYT essay got accused over them).
6. Hedged diplomatic balance ("While X, Y is also important").
7. Significance inflation ("marking a pivotal moment," trailing -ing clauses).
8. Bold-header-colon listicles, "In conclusion" wrap-ups.
9. Fake experience — generic scenarios, default names, no proper nouns.
10. Perfect grammar, zero risk, no contractions, no opinions that cost anything.
- Mental model: PATTERN DENSITY (tells per 100 words). One tell is innocent; clustering convicts. (The Novelist Studio, May 2026.)

### Top 10 techniques (attributed)
1. Kill negative parallelism + tricolons on sight (Wikipedia signs page).
2. Engineer burstiness — long winding sentence, then a two-word one (Olivia Cal "high-low"). Adam's staccato already does this; protect it.
3. Specifics only you possess — names, dates, the truck cab at 4 a.m. (GPTZero guide, Fordham).
4. Write things that could cost you (Tim Denning).
5. First person, relevant tangents, anecdotes (Evan Edinger).
6. Embrace mess — fragments, incomplete thoughts (Denning).
7. Pub test — read aloud (Cal).
8. Delete throat-clearing intros and summary conclusions (Pangram).
9. AI to interrogate yourself / surface anecdotes, never to generate prose (David Perell workflow, every.to; perell.com/ai).
10. Don't overcorrect into blandness — that's its own tell (SALT.agency).

## PART 2 — SUBSTACK LENGTH / PAYWALL / CADENCE

### Length
- No credible word-count-to-conversion study exists. Consensus range 800–1,200 general; 1,200–1,800 engagement zone; deep analysis 2,000–4,000.
- Best conversion-specific data point: Product Compass ($156,960/yr, 1,962 paid): 2,000–3,000-word posts with 1,000+ words FREE above the paywall (Pubstack Success).
- Newsletter Circle bestseller study: 800–1,000-word free preview that is already valuable, cliff at peak interest.
- Gmail clips ~102KB HTML — put upgrade CTA early, before clip point.

### Paywall model verdict
- Mid-post paywall (preview + cliff) = dominant bestseller pattern (Lenny, Simmons, Kramer).
- Simon Owens: revealing too much cut subscription odds 86.3%/72.2%; early cliff converts, deep cliff grows reach — deliberate tradeoff.
- BM's free-for-3-days-then-archive-paywall: NO source anywhere shows it converts as a primary strategy. Structural flaw: the EMAIL arrives fully free, so the engaged reader never hits a cliff at peak interest. Only archive latecomers hit the wall. Steve Skojec ran it and reversed it (skojecfile).
- Keep archive paywall as backstop; conversion should come from designated paid posts with generous mid-post previews.

### Cadence
- Substack official: at least 1/week per audience (free + paid).
- Finn Tropy, 2.3M posts / 13,534 creators: 100–999 paid tier averages 2.64 posts/wk; 1,000–9,999 paid tier averages 3.45/wk. Frequency correlates with revenue but tops out ~4.4/wk even at 10K+ paid.
- Lenny's ($2M/yr) publishes 2/wk and CUT BACK after "too much content" feedback. Pragmatic Engineer ~3/wk. Noahpinion ~5/wk (3 free, 2 paid).
- HubSpot: 34% unsubscribe when email exceeds once daily.

### Free:paid ratio + conversion benchmarks
- Most-cited: 3 free : 1 paid (attributed to Substack data). Common minimum 1 free + 1 paid weekly. Noahpinion 3:2.
- Real free→paid medians: ~3%, normal 2–5%; only ~20% of pubs exceed 5%. BM at 5.1% is ALREADY above the real-world median.
- Channel matters most: Notes/app-sourced subs ~9.3% conversion vs imported social ~1.2%.
