All figures current as of June 2026. Concrete numbers cited; estimates and unverified claims flagged VERIFY.

## CONTEXT: the platform fee you pay first (changes the ad math)
beehiiv is not free for a 25K list. You must be on the **Scale plan** to access ANY monetization (Ad Network, Boosts, paid subs, referral program). Tiered by subscriber count:

This monthly fee is beehiiv's whole business model — it's WHY they take 0% of your revenue. The fee is the trade for the percentage cut.

## (1) AD NETWORK — the sponsor money
**How it works:** beehiiv runs a marketplace. They source advertisers, pre-negotiate campaigns, hand you ad creative (image/copy/link), you claim offers and drop them into your sends. They track performance and pay you. You can claim up to 5 ads at once (120-hour window to publish), max 3 ads per post. Payouts arrive on the **20th of each month** for the prior month's ads. Reports land ~96 hours after send (clicks run through extra verification). Sources: https://www.beehiiv.com/support/article/17507491038231-ad-network-faq , https://www.beehiiv.com/support/article/17525818225431-using-the-beehiiv-ad-network

**Who qualifies:** Two different requirements appear:
- beehiiv's own pages say only: "available to anyone on a paid plan (Scale or above) who is actively sending." No hard subscriber minimum stated on official pages.
- Third-party sources cite a **1,000 active subscriber minimum + 20%+ open rate** and a list-quality review. Source: https://rooknows.com/beehiiv-is-paying-creators-1m-per-month-how-to-get-your-cut-in-2026/
- At ~25K subs you clear any version of this easily. The open-rate/list-quality bar matters — VERIFY your current open rate is 20%+ (Substack-migrated lists sometimes dip on first sends).

**Payment models:** CPM (per 1,000 unique opens) or CPC (per verified click). beehiiv's own examples: "$5 CPM = $5 per 1,000 unique opens"; "$2 CPC × 100 verified clicks = $200." These are illustrative, NOT rate cards. Source: https://www.beehiiv.com/features/ad-network/publishers

**Does beehiiv take 0% of Ad Network revenue?** — VERIFY / IMPORTANT NUANCE. beehiiv's "0% take rate" marketing applies explicitly to **paid subscriptions**, not the Ad Network. None of beehiiv's official Ad Network pages state a 0% take or a 100%-to-publisher split. The CPM/CPC you see in the dashboard is the net rate beehiiv pays you; beehiiv keeps the spread between what the advertiser pays and what they pay you (standard ad-network model). So treat "beehiiv takes 0% of ad revenue" as **FALSE/unconfirmed** — the dashboard number is already net to you, with beehiiv's margin baked in upstream. Sources checked: https://www.beehiiv.com/support/article/17507491038231-ad-network-faq , https://www.beehiiv.com/features/ad-network/publishers (neither discloses a publisher revenue split)

**Realistic RPM for a 25K Christian-men list:**
- General-interest/lifestyle/faith content sits at the **LOW end** of CPM ranges. Finance, B2B, SaaS, investing command the premiums ($30–100/sub/year); faith/men's-interest does not. Source: https://www.beehiiv.com/blog/how-much-money-can-you-make-from-1-000-newsletter-subscribers-in-2025
- Quoted CPM range: **$5–20 CPM**; for a niche non-finance list, realistically assume **$5–12 CPM**. VERIFY against your actual dashboard after 30 days — beehiiv explicitly says this is the only reliable estimate.
- **Math for 25K subs:** At a 40% open rate = 10,000 unique opens per send.
  - $5 CPM → $50/send
  - $10 CPM → $100/send
  - $12 CPM → $120/send
- With up to 3 ad slots per send, the per-send number can roughly multiply if you fill slots and advertisers are available (fill rate is NOT guaranteed — Christian-men is a thinner advertiser pool than finance, so expect partial fill). VERIFY fill rate.
- **Realistic monthly range, weekly send, single ad slot, partial fill:** roughly **$150–500/mo** at 25K. Optimistic (multi-slot, full fill, higher open rate): up to ~$1,000+/mo. These are estimates — VERIFY.

With ~25K total / 1,301 paid, even a few percent of paid subscribers missing emails during warmup is direct revenue risk. And every stale address you bring over actively HURTS the warmup. You'd want to scrub the list first, which means accepting you'll re-import a SMALLER list than you have today.

# MIGRATION MECHANICS & RISK-REDUCTION — Substack → beehiiv (current as of June 2026)

## 1. WHAT THE BEEHIIV IMPORT TOOL TRANSFERS

beehiiv has a built-in Substack importer (Settings > Content Import, or Settings > Publication > Import Content; the older path is Settings > Content Import > Start Import). Content import runs off your public Substack URL and takes ~5–10 min; subscriber import is a separate CSV upload.

**Transfers cleanly:**
- **Free subscribers** — via Substack's `email_list.csv` export. Carries email, name, signup date, country, source, and Substack's "activity rating" engagement signal; tags/custom fields map during import.
- **Posts/archive** — free posts pull automatically from your Substack URL; paid posts come from Substack's exported ZIP.
- **Podcast episodes** — archive importable; RSS can redirect to beehiiv's feed.

**Does NOT transfer (you recreate manually):**
- Comments — gone.
- Paywall placements — paid posts import **fully gated** to non-paid; you re-add paywall blocks by hand.
- Video posts, podcast-type posts (separate manual flow), photo galleries, category structures, embeds.
- Code blocks import as plain text (recreate via HTML Snippet).

## 2. PAID SUBSCRIBERS — THE $82K RISK (this is the load-bearing finding)

**The good news, and it materially lowers the risk:** Both Substack and beehiiv run paid subs on **Stripe**. beehiiv's importer copies your customers' **PAN (card) data** from your source Stripe account into a **new beehiiv-connected Stripe account** via Stripe's PCI-compliant copy process. **Subscribers do NOT re-enter their cards and do NOT have to re-subscribe.** This is fundamentally different from migrating to a platform that forces re-subscribe.

Mechanics, all from the official beehiiv paid-import doc:
- You connect a **new Stripe account** to beehiiv (Stripe auto-detects your existing Substack-connected business and pre-fills). Paid subs are **recreated** in this new account. beehiiv uses a *personal Stripe account* for your subscriber revenue (separate from the Express accounts it uses for its own plan billing / ad network).
- The PAN copy "may take **up to 72 hours** depending on size, though it is generally much faster."
- Imported subs are placed on a **temporary trial until their normal renewal date** — prevents double-billing during the card-data copy.
- **Grandfathered pricing:** every imported sub keeps their current price regardless of which beehiiv tier you map them to (a $3/mo legacy sub mapped to a $5 tier stays at $3; only NEW signups pay $5). No surprise price changes.
- **You must manually cancel/pause billing on Substack.** beehiiv does NOT do this. If you skip it, subs get charged on BOTH platforms.

**Quantified risk to the 1,242 active paid / ~$82K ARR:**
- Because cards copy and subs don't re-confirm, the catastrophic "everyone must re-subscribe" scenario does **not** apply here. The realistic loss is from (a) **expired/failed cards** that would have failed anyway, (b) **iOS/Apple-billed and comped/gifted subs** which do NOT carry a Stripe card — these must be hand-granted comp access, and any iOS-billed paid subs are effectively at risk, and (c) any sub whose card the copy can't validate.
- **VERIFY (cannot confirm a current rate live):** A re-confirmation/attrition figure of ~5–15% appears in secondary write-ups, but that figure applies to platforms that force re-entry. For the Stripe-PAN-copy path, expected hard loss should be materially lower — bounded mainly by failed cards + Apple-billed subs. **Action item: pull your Substack subscriber report and count how many paid subs are iOS/Apple-billed or comped vs. Stripe-card-on-file before committing — that number is your true at-risk population, not the 1,242.**

Sources: [beehiiv: How to import paid subscribers](https://www.beehiiv.com/support/article/12231121444759-how-to-import-paid-subscribers) · [beehiiv: Stripe account types](https://www.beehiiv.com/support/article/30064926230679-using-stripe-with-beehiiv-account-types-explained) · [Stripe: PAN/data migration (PCI process)](https://docs.stripe.com/get-started/data-migrations/pan-import) — note: Stripe's public PAN doc describes processor→Stripe; beehiiv's account-to-account copy is the operative path and is documented in the beehiiv article above.

## 3. CUSTOM DOMAIN + DELIVERABILITY WARMUP (25K subs, new platform)

**Domain:** beehiiv partners with **Entri** for DNS — no manual DNS edits in most cases. Set up your custom sending domain and configure **SPF + DKIM before sending anything** (non-negotiable, first step).

**Smart Warming (beehiiv's automated reputation builder):**
- Auto-triggers for publications sending to >100 subs; if the custom domain is set up early, it activates at ~200 subs.
- For senders shipping >1x/week, warming **typically takes 6–8 weeks**; slower cadence takes longer.
- **Large-import penalty:** if an import adds ≥200 subs AND grows your list by ≥50%, Smart Warming **extends** to absorb the new volume. (For Adam this is less punishing — 25K is the existing list, not 50%+ growth — but a cold 25K on a brand-new domain still needs the ramp.)

Sending fresh mail from a new domain to 25K cold contacts is the single biggest spam-foldering risk.
