# you are the one it stops with

My grandfather drank.

My father carried what the drink left behind.

I carried what my father couldn't put down.

You know this math. You did it as a kid. You watched a man you loved hand you a wound he never asked for. And you swore you'd be different.

Then you grew up. And you heard his voice come out of your own mouth.

That is the part nobody warns you about.

The Bible already named it. "Visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation." Exodus says it plain. The sin travels. It doesn't ask permission. It moves down the bloodline like a name on a deed.

You inherited a house you never bought.

And here is the verse that ruined me at 3 AM. You are standing in that house right now. Your kids are asleep down the hall. And the chain is in your hands.

It stops with you. Or it doesn't stop.

That's the whole job.

Not the career. Not the church attendance record. The chain. The thing your father handed you that you refuse to hand to your son.

A reader wrote me this week. He said, "Breaking it takes more than most men will ever spend." He's right. It costs everything. Sleep. Pride. The version of you that wanted to coast.

Most men won't pay it. They manage the curse instead. They drink a little less than dad did and call it victory. They yell a little quieter. They check out a little slower.

That isn't breaking the chain. That's loosening it so it rattles less.

God didn't call you to a quieter chain.

Joshua stood in front of a whole nation and drew a line in the dirt. "As for me and my house, we will serve the LORD." He didn't poll his family. He didn't wait for consensus. He declared the bloodline's direction and dared anyone to argue.

That's a man being the head of his house.

You don't break a generational curse with a feeling. You break it with a decision you make again every single morning before anyone wakes up.

I make it on the couch at 4 AM. Bible open. Coffee black. Five kids asleep who have no idea their father is at war for them.

They'll never see this fight. Good. That's the point.

The men whose fathers fought never knew it either. They just woke up free and assumed everybody lived that way.

Free is the inheritance you build by hand.

So here's the line in the dirt for you today.

The thing you swore you'd never become. The temper. The bottle. The screen. The silence that you learned from a man who learned it from his.

Name it. Out loud. To God.

Then decide it dies in your generation. Not managed. Not medicated. Dead.

You are the one it stops with.

Nobody behind you can do it. Nobody after you should have to.

"Choose you this day whom ye will serve."

Choose. Then go wake your kids.
