# they shrunk the flood on purpose

You were handed a bathtub and told it was the end of the world.

Two giraffes. A smiling lion. A rainbow. A boat the size of a backyard shed.

They made Noah's ark a nursery decoration.

Ask the cubit question and the cartoon dies.

How big was the ark? Wrong question. How big was the cubit?

The royal cubit changes the math. The boat grows. The shed becomes a structure longer than a football field, taller than a building, riding water that buried the mountains.

But they did not shrink the ark by accident.

Shrink the ark and you shrink the flood. Shrink the flood and you shrink the judgment. Shrink the judgment and you shrink the God who sent it.

That is the whole game.

A small flood is a fairy tale. A small fairy tale asks nothing of you. You can hang it on a nursery wall and sleep fine.

A flood that drowned a world is a warning.

"As it was in the days of Noe, so shall it be also in the days of the Son of man." He said it Himself. Not a metaphor. A pattern. A second one is coming, and it will not be water.

The men in Noah's day were not monsters in a movie.

They ate. They drank. They married. They scrolled their version of the feed. They heard the hammer for 120 years and called it noise.

Then the door shut.

Not Noah's hand on it. God's.

You were taught to picture a toy so you would never picture the door.

The cartoon is not innocent. The cartoon is anesthesia.

The ark was enormous. The flood was total. The judgment was real.

And the next one has your generation's name written across the sky.
