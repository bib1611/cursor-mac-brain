# When the Shepherd Bites

Her pastor tried to cast Jezebel out of her.

In front of the small group.

After prayer.

She wrote me that this week. Said she still tries to forgive him. Said it hurt her so deeply she has to lay it down every single morning.

That is not deliverance.

That is assault dressed in scripture.

And the worst part is the silence after. The friends who watched. The husband who froze. The leader who walked away clean while she carried the scar.

She thought she was the problem.

That is the lie.

## The Verse They Used

Touch not mine anointed, and do my prophets no harm. Psalm 105:15.

They hand you that verse like a muzzle.

Speak up about the wound and you are touching God's man. Question the method and you are quenching the Spirit. Cry in the parking lot and you are bitter.

That verse is about Israel. About kings. About a nation God protected from foreign powers. It was never a shield for a man with a microphone and a wandering hand.

Read the chapter. The whole one. Not the bumper sticker.

## What the Bible Actually Says About Bad Shepherds

Ezekiel 34. Read it tonight.

Woe be to the shepherds of Israel that do feed themselves. Should not the shepherds feed the flocks?

The diseased have ye not strengthened, neither have ye healed that which was sick, neither have ye bound up that which was broken, neither have ye brought again that which was driven away, neither have ye sought that which was lost. But with force and with cruelty have ye ruled them.

That is not a soft passage.

That is God indicting the men who hurt His sheep.

Behold, I am against the shepherds.

Read that line twice.

## What He Did Was Not God

When the man at the front of the room turned your wound into a demonstration, that was not the Holy Ghost. That was a script. He had to perform power because he had no real authority. He had to make you the spectacle so no one looked at him.

You were the prop.

You were not the patient.

He was not healing. He was hunting applause.

## The Difference Between a Father and a Predator

A father in the faith covers the wound. A predator parades it.

A father whispers correction. A predator broadcasts it.

A father lifts the head. A predator pushes it down.

A father stays after the meeting. A predator leaves you to clean up the chairs.

You can tell the difference by what you feel when his car pulls into the lot. Peace, or panic.

## The God Who Sees What He Did

Thou God seest me. Genesis 16:13.

Hagar said that. Pregnant. Abandoned. Running into the wilderness because the household that was supposed to keep her had cast her out.

God found her at a well.

He did not ask her to forget what was done. He did not ask her to repent of her tears. He sent her back with a name and a promise and an angel.

He sees you in the small group too.

He saw the laying on of hands that should not have been there.

He saw the prayer turned weapon.

He saw the room go silent when you needed someone to say stop.

He has not forgotten any of it.

## The Way Out Is Not Another Building

I am not telling you to never go to church again. I am telling you to stop going to that one.

You can love the body of Christ and still walk away from a wolf in the pulpit. You can honor the office and still rebuke the man.

Find a church where the pastor knows your kids' names. Where the preaching cuts you but does not crush you. Where the leader can be asked a question without his face going red. Where the women are not props for the men's spiritual theater.

They exist. They are quieter than the famous ones.

Look for the quiet ones.

## The Last Thing

If a man laid hands on you and called it deliverance and left you worse, hear this:

That was not your moment of weakness. That was his.

You did not fail a test. You met a fraud.

The God who heals does not need a stage.

He is in the car on the way home. He is in the 3 AM ceiling. He is in the verse you cannot stop turning over in your head while you do the dishes.

He never left.

He never lifted His hand against you.

The shepherd bit you.

The Shepherd is binding you up.
