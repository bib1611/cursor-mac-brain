# The Lie You Believed Before You Sinned

The sin was not the first thing.

The lie was.

You did the thing because you believed the thing.

You believed the thing because somebody whispered it before you knew how to read.

## What Eve Did Before She Ate

Read Genesis 3 again.

The serpent did not hand her the fruit.

He handed her a sentence.

"Yea, hath God said, Ye shall not eat of every tree of the garden?"

That is the line.

That is the whole story of every man's fall written in one verse.

He did not say "eat."

He said "did God really say."

The mouth got busy with the fruit only after the ear had already swallowed the lie.

## The Lie Comes First. Every Time.

You did not look at the screen because you wanted the screen.

You looked at the screen because somewhere in the dark a sentence got planted in you that said "you are alone and nobody is coming and this is the only way you will feel anything tonight."

You did not yell at your wife because you wanted to yell at your wife.

You yelled because a sentence had been running in your head for nineteen years that said "she does not respect you and if you do not raise your voice you will disappear."

You did not drink because you wanted the drink.

You drank because a sentence was sitting in your chest that said "God left a long time ago and you are on your own and this is what men like you do at the end of the day."

The sin is the fruit.

The lie is the root.

You keep cutting at the fruit and wondering why the tree is still standing.

## The Lie I Believed at Thirteen

A man hurt me when I was thirteen.

He did the act. That was the sin done to me.

But he also handed me a sentence on his way out.

"This is your fault. Nobody will believe you. If you say anything, you will be the one who burns."

I carried that sentence for thirty one years.

I set fire to my school six months later because the sentence said I was already burning. May as well make it real.

I drove a garbage truck at twenty below for years because the sentence said I did not deserve a desk.

I wrote at 3 AM with the door locked for years because the sentence said if anybody saw what was inside me, they would leave.

The lie was loud the whole time. I just did not know it was a lie.

I thought it was me.

## What David Saw

Psalm 51:6.

"Behold, thou desirest truth in the inward parts."

The inward parts.

Not the lips. Not the resume. Not the church seat.

The basement. The place underneath the place. The room where the sentences live.

God is not after your behavior first.

He is after the sentence underneath the behavior.

Because He knows the only way you stop doing the thing is to stop believing the thing.

## Donna Said It Plain

A reader named Donna wrote this week.

"I had to go all the way back to what lie I believed over truth. That's usually the root of it. I had to repent for believing a lie and replace it with the Truth of God's Word. Took me many years to understand this pattern."

Read that twice.

She did not repent of the sin first.

She repented of the lie underneath the sin.

Then she replaced the lie with a verse.

That is the order. That has always been the order. The Reformers ran on that order. The Puritans died on that order. David lived on that order. Jesus modeled it in the wilderness with three "it is written" verses in a row against three lies in a row.

The sword is the truth.

The serpent only has one weapon.

A sentence.

## How to Find the Lie

Get in a chair tonight.

Take the sin you keep going back to.

Do not start at the sin.

Start at the moment right before the sin.

Ask the Lord, "What was I believing the second before I did that?"

Sit there.

Do not fill the silence.

He will surface the sentence.

It will sound like your father. It will sound like your sixth grade teacher. It will sound like the kid at the lunch table. It will sound like the man who hurt you. It will sound like the wife who left. It will sound like your own voice from ten years ago.

You will recognize it the second it shows up.

You have been quoting it to yourself for decades.

## What to Do With the Sentence

Two things.

One. Say it out loud and name it as a lie.

"I am alone." Lie.

"Nobody is coming." Lie.

"God left when I did the thing." Lie.

"This is who I am." Lie.

"It is too late." Lie.

Speaking it out loud breaks half its power. Demons hate plain English.

Two. Find the verse that says the opposite and learn it by heart.

Not skim. Not screenshot. Learn it. Mouth it on the drive home until it owns the same airspace the lie has been owning for nineteen years.

"I will never leave thee, nor forsake thee." Hebrews 13:5.

"There hath no temptation taken you but such as is common to man." 1 Corinthians 10:13.

"He that hath begun a good work in you will perform it." Philippians 1:6.

"Behold, I am with you alway, even unto the end of the world." Matthew 28:20.

Pick one and put it where the lie used to live.

The new sentence has to be louder than the old one.

## What This Looks Like in Six Months

You stop cutting at the fruit.

You start swinging at the root.

The screen still calls but it sounds quieter because the sentence underneath it just got buried under Hebrews 13:5.

The wife still misunderstands but the sentence in your chest is no longer "she will leave me," it is "I have a covenant and God keeps covenants and so do I."

The drink still sits on the shelf but the sentence is no longer "God already left," it is "the Lord is at my right hand, I shall not be moved."

Same temptations. Different basement.

Different basement.

Different man.

## The Last Thing

There is a sentence sitting in you tonight.

It is the engine under every sin you cannot kill.

Name it.

Bury it.

Plant a verse on top of it.

Then go to bed.

The fight is not at the fruit.

The fight has always been at the sentence.
