# BM Note — 2026-06-24

My grandfather drank.

My father carried what the drink left behind.

I carried what neither could put down.

You know this math. You did it as a kid.

Then you grew up. And you heard his voice come out of your own mouth.

The Bible named it. The iniquity of the fathers, visited unto the third and fourth generation. The sin doesn't ask permission. It moves down the bloodline like a name on a deed.

You inherited a house you never bought.

But the chain is in your hands now.

It stops with you. Or it doesn't stop.

Most men just manage it. They drink a little less than dad did and call it victory. That's not breaking the chain. That's loosening it so it rattles less.

Name the thing you swore you'd never become. Out loud. To God.

Then decide it dies in your generation.

You are the one it stops with.
