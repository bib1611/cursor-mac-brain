# the book in your hands is wet with blood

Pick up your Bible.

Feel the weight of it.

A man was strangled and burned for that weight.

His name was William Tyndale.

1536. Belgium. Tied to a stake.

His crime: he put the words of God into English so the plowboy could read them.

Before Tyndale, the Book was chained. Latin only. Priest only.

You came to God through a middleman or you did not come at all.

That was the system. That was the point.

Tyndale looked a learned man in the eye and said it plain.

"If God spare my life, ere many years I will cause a boy that driveth the plough shall know more of the Scripture than thou dost."

They hunted him for that one sentence.

He finished anyway. Translated in hiding. Smuggled the pages in bales of cloth.

They caught him. Strangled him. Burned the body.

His last words were a prayer. "Lord, open the King of England's eyes."

Three years later the King ordered an English Bible placed in every church.

The prayer outlived the fire.

Here is what the soft Sunday version never tells you.

The fight for your Bible was a war. Bodies in it. Ash in it.

And the war never stopped. It only changed clothes.

They don't burn translators now. They don't have to.

They hand you ten thousand versions until the words go numb.

They give you a phone that glows brighter than the Book.

They raise your children on screens and let the Bible sit closed on the shelf.

A closed Bible is a chained Bible.

It does not matter that it is free if you never open it.

The men who died to put it in your language are watching dust gather on the cover.

Old Deluder Satan. That is what the Puritans named the enemy in 1647.

The deluder who wanted men kept away from the Scriptures.

So they passed a law. Every child would learn to read. One reason given. So Satan could not keep them in the dark.

They understood something we forgot.

"The entrance of thy words giveth light." (Psalm 119:130)

Light you keep switched off.

He lost the battle to chain the Book. He is winning the battle to make you ignore it.

Open it tonight.

Not the app. The Book.

Read it like a man died to hand it to you.

Because one did.
