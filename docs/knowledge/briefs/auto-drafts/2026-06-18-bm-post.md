# The Donkey God Sent to Save You

You are beating the wrong animal.

You have been beating it for years.

You think it is the obstacle. You think it is the inconvenience. You think it is the reason your life is not moving the way you wanted it to move.

It is the mercy.

## Numbers 22

Balaam saddled his donkey to go where God told him not to go.

The angel of the Lord stood in the road with a sword drawn.

Balaam did not see the angel.

The donkey did.

The donkey turned aside into a field.

Balaam beat her.

The donkey crushed his foot against a wall.

Balaam beat her.

The donkey laid down underneath him and refused to move.

Balaam beat her with a staff.

Three times the donkey saved his life and three times he beat the only creature on earth standing between him and the sword.

Then the Lord opened the donkey's mouth and she said the line that should sit in every man's chest for the rest of his life.

"What have I done unto thee, that thou hast smitten me these three times?"

## The Donkey Is Almost Never What You Think

The donkey in your life is not the enemy.

The donkey is your wife when she will not let you walk back into the sin.

The donkey is the friend who keeps bringing up the verse you do not want to hear.

The donkey is the pastor who looked at you on Tuesday and said the one sentence you needed and you have been avoiding his calls ever since.

The donkey is the layoff that stopped you from buying the house that would have buried you.

The donkey is the illness that took you off the road and put you in the chair where you finally opened the Book.

The donkey is the daughter who will not stop praying for you.

The donkey is the friend who will not stop telling you to stop drinking.

The donkey is the obstruction that is the answer.

You have been beating it.

## What Grace Looks Like to a Man in a Hurry

Grace does not always look like a gift.

Sometimes grace looks like a wall.

Sometimes grace looks like a field your donkey turned into instead of the road you wanted to take.

Sometimes grace looks like your foot getting crushed against a stone.

Balaam wanted to go. The angel was there to keep him from going. The donkey was the only one with eyes to see it.

You wanted to go.

The Lord sent a wall.

You called it bad luck.

## What the Donkey Was Risking

Every time she turned aside, she took the staff.

She did not turn aside because she wanted to humiliate him.

She turned aside because the alternative was watching her master die on a sword he could not see.

She took the beating to save the man who was beating her.

That is half the people in your life right now.

The wife taking your anger because she will not let you go back to the screen.

The friend taking your silent treatment because he will not stop telling you the truth.

The pastor taking your departure from his church because he will not soften the verse for your itch.

The Lord Himself taking your blame for the wall He put in front of you.

He is not the obstacle.

He is the sword you cannot see.

## What Happened the Moment Balaam Saw

The angel said, "the ass saw me, and turned from me these three times: unless she had turned from me, surely now also I had slain thee, and saved her alive."

Read that sentence twice.

The donkey saved his life.

He was beating the only creature standing between him and his own funeral.

Then Balaam fell on his face.

Forty four years and a sword in the road and that is what it took.

## The Question You Have to Ask Tonight

Who in your life have you been beating because they keep getting in your way?

Name the person.

Name the situation.

Name the wall.

The closed door at the company. The chronic illness. The bank account that will not let you make the move. The wife. The friend. The deacon. The kid. The father in law.

Look at it again.

It might not be the obstacle.

It might be the donkey.

## What I Learned the Hard Way

I have a wife who has refused to let me die quietly more times than I have the room to write.

For a long time I called her difficult.

For a long time I called her too sensitive.

For a long time I called her the reason my plans were not working.

She was the donkey.

She was the wall.

She was the angel with the sword that I could not see standing on the road I was trying to walk down.

I beat her with silence and with sarcasm and with leaving the room and with twenty other small staffs a man uses when he does not want to be told the truth.

She kept taking it.

She kept turning aside.

She kept laying down underneath the weight of a man who was about to ride himself into death and call it freedom.

Twenty four years later I am still here because she would not move.

## What This Does Not Mean

It does not mean you call every obstacle a divine wall.

Some obstacles are the devil. Some are your own sin. Some are the consequence catching up with the choice.

But the men I know are not in danger of mistaking the devil for a friend.

We are in danger of mistaking the friend for the devil.

We are in danger of mistaking the mercy for the enemy.

We are in danger of beating the donkey for three miles and finding out at the end of the road that she was the only thing keeping us from the sword.

## The Last Thing

Repent to the donkey tonight.

Whoever she is.

Whatever she is.

The wife. The friend. The boss. The illness. The wall.

Get on your face the way Balaam did and tell the Lord you have been beating the mercy for years and you did not know.

He already knew.

He is the One who sent the donkey.

He has been waiting for you to see the sword.
