# the sin you didn't start

You didn't start it.

Your father handed it to you. He got it from his father. Back and back, a chain you never forged.

The temper. The bottle. The silence at the dinner table.

The way men in your family disappear into work and never come home, even when they are sitting right there.

You inherited it like an old coat. Too big when you were a boy. It fits now.

The Bible saw this before you were born. "Visiting the iniquity of the fathers upon the children unto the third and fourth generation." Exodus said it flat. Sin runs in bloodlines. It does.

So you call it fate. You think the coat is your skin.

It isn't.

Ezekiel got handed the other half. "The son shall not bear the iniquity of the father." Same Book. No contradiction. The iniquity visits. The iniquity does not have to stay.

Here is the part nobody tells you.

The chain breaks on a generation that decides to bleed instead of pass it down.

That generation can be yours.

It costs you something. It costs you the 3 AM honesty you have been dodging. It costs you naming the thing your father never named. It costs you sitting in the discomfort instead of pouring a drink or opening a tab or raising your voice.

Your kids are watching which coat you wear.

They will wear the same one. Or they will watch you take it off, fold it, and bury it. They learn repentance by watching a man repent. They learn nothing from a man who pretends the coat was always his skin.

I carried things 31 years in silence.

Silence is not breaking the chain. Silence is oiling it.

"Be not overcome of evil, but overcome evil with good." That is not a poster. That is a command to the man holding the coat.

Take it off.

The sin you didn't start, you can finish.

Not your father. You.

It stops with us.
