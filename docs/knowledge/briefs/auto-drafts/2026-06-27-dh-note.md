# DH Substack Note — 2026-06-27

Pick up your Bible. Feel the weight of it.

A man was strangled and burned for that weight. His name was William Tyndale. 1536. Tied to a stake for putting the words of God into English so a plowboy could read them.

Before him the Book was chained. Latin only. Priest only. You came through a middleman or you didn't come at all.

They don't burn translators now. They don't have to. They hand you a phone that glows brighter than the Book and let it sit closed on the shelf.

A closed Bible is a chained Bible. Open it tonight. Not the app. The Book. Read it like a man died to hand it to you.

Because one did.
