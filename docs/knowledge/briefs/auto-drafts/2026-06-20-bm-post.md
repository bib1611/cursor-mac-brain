# The Oldest Question in the World

3 AM. The house is quiet.

The doubt is not.

It does not shout. It whispers. It always has.

Did God really say that?

That is the first question in the whole story after the garden was called good. The serpent did not open with a lie. He opened with a question.

Yea, hath God said? Genesis 3:1.

He did not rip the page out of her hand. He just made her squint at it.

That is still the entire game.

## He Never Changed His Method

He does not need you to burn the Book.

He only needs you to mistrust one line of it.

One verse. One whisper. One "well, that probably means something a little different in the original."

And the muzzle is on.

Doubt the word and you stop standing on it. You stand beside it. You hover over it like a man who is not sure the ice will hold.

I know that hover. I have stood there.

The verse is right in front of me and the voice in the dark says, are you really sure that says what you think it says.

That voice is old. That voice has a name.

## The Word Did Not Come to You Beat Up

The words of the LORD are pure words: as silver tried in a furnace of earth, purified seven times. Psalm 12:6.

Read the next line. Most people skip it.

Thou shalt keep them, O LORD, thou shalt preserve them from this generation for ever. Psalm 12:7.

He did not just speak it. He swore to keep it.

So when the whisper comes asking if God really said it, I am not arguing about a manuscript anymore.

I am being asked if God can keep a promise.

That is the real question under the question.

## What I Do at 3 AM Now

I do not win the debate with the serpent. Eve tried that. She lost.

I just open the Book and read it out loud.

Not to prove it. To plant my feet on it.

All scripture is given by inspiration of God. 2 Timothy 3:16. Given. Not assembled by a committee that hopes it got close.

The grass withereth, the flower fadeth: but the word of our God shall stand for ever. Isaiah 40:8.

It will outlast the whisper. It will outlast the scholar. It will outlast me.

## The Command

Stop hovering.

Stop letting one slick question talk you off the only solid ground you have.

When the dark asks if God really said it, do not answer the dark.

Answer it with the verse.

Out loud. Tonight.

He kept His word for you. Now keep your feet on it.
