# the silence of adam

Read it again. Slower this time.

"She gave also unto her husband with her; and he did eat."

With her.

He was standing there. The whole time.

We were told a story about a woman and a snake. A weak wife. A clever devil. The man shows up late, gets tricked by his bride, takes the bite. Poor Adam. Wrong place, wrong fruit.

That's the cartoon. Burn it.

He was there for the conversation. There when the serpent asked the question. There when the lie unspooled in his own garden, aimed at the woman God gave him to guard.

And the first man said nothing.

No rebuke. No warning. No standing between her and the thing in the grass. He let hell run its mouth in his own house and he kept quiet, because silence was easier than war.

Then he ate. And the world broke.

The first sin wasn't a bite. It was a man who would not speak.

A reader put it plain this week. He named a book. The Silence of Adam. And he asked the question that should keep every man up at night. Would we have done any of those things? Spoken to the serpent. Called out to God. Stood in the gap.

We know the answer. We do it every day.

The serpent still talks. He just changed venues. He whispers to your wife through a screen at midnight. He raises your children with a curriculum you never approved. He moves through your house like a draft through old wood, and you feel the cold, and you say nothing, because saying something means a fight.

So you eat. Whatever's handed to you. The lie. The peace that isn't peace. The quiet that costs you everything.

Passive men don't keep the garden. They lose it.

Hear this. The devil is not afraid of a busy man. He's afraid of a man who opens his mouth. A man who reads the Word out loud at his own table. A man who looks at the thing in the grass and says, not in this house.

Adam had a sword he never drew. So do you.

"Watch ye, stand fast in the faith, quit you like men, be strong."

Quit you like men. Stop being furniture in your own home.

The silence of Adam cost us Eden. Your silence is costing you a household, one quiet night at a time.

Open your mouth.

The garden is already crawling.
