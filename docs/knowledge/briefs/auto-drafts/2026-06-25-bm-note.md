put the phone down.

your kid is right there. and he can tell the difference between being glanced at and being looked at. every kid can.

i drove a garbage truck for years. then a train. came home tired and called it love. the bills were paid, so i thought that was the whole job.

it wasn't.

a man can be home and gone at the same time. body in the chair. soul somewhere else. the kids grow up next to a ghost who pays the rent.

"lo, children are an heritage of the Lord." an heritage. not an interruption.

look up. you were in the room the whole time. be in it.
