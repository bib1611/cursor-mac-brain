# faithful when the tank is empty

Some mornings I open the laptop with nothing.

No fire. No words. No want.

Just the cursor blinking. And me.

A reader wrote me this week. Said he could sense I was struggling. Said he was praying for me.

He was right.

Some weeks press harder than others.

I used to think faith was a feeling. A warmth. A lift. Goosebumps in the third verse.

Then I read about Elijah.

He had just called fire down from heaven. Killed four hundred prophets of Baal. The biggest day of his whole ministry.

And the very next chapter he is under a juniper tree begging to die.

"It is enough; now, O LORD, take away my life; for I am not better than my fathers." (1 Kings 19:4)

The man who out-prayed an entire false religion wanted to quit the next morning.

That is not weak faith. That is a body that ran out of fuel.

God did not rebuke him. Read it for yourself.

He let him sleep. Sent an angel. Fed him bread and water. Twice.

"Arise and eat; because the journey is too great for thee." (1 Kings 19:7)

Not "fix your attitude." Not "claim your victory."

Eat. Sleep. Get up.

We built a Christianity that shames the empty man.

If you really walked with God, you wouldn't feel this way.

Lie. Straight from the pit.

Some of the most faithful men in the Bible wanted out.

Moses. "Kill me, I pray thee, out of hand." (Numbers 11:15)

Job cursed the day he was born. Jonah sat down to die. David soaked his couch with tears.

Faithfulness is not the absence of the empty morning.

Faithfulness is showing up to the empty morning.

You don't feel like leading your family. Lead them anyway.

You don't feel like opening the Book. Open it anyway.

You don't feel saved. You're still saved.

The feeling is weather. The covenant is ground.

I showed up today with nothing. And here are the words.

Not because I am strong.

Because He fed me bread when I wanted to die under the tree.

Get up. Eat. The journey is too great to quit on an empty stomach.

He is not done with you.

He wasn't done with Elijah either.
