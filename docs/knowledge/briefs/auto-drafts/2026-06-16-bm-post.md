# The Donkey Was the Mercy

A reader wrote me yesterday.

He said it like a confession. "Trying to kill the one who is trying to save you."

He was reading Numbers 22.

He saw what most men miss.

## The Story Nobody Wants

Balaam wanted the money.

The princes of Moab came with rewards in their hands. They wanted the prophet to curse Israel. He said let me ask God again. God already told him no. He asked again because he wanted a different answer.

You have done that.

I have done that.

We ask God twice when the first answer cuts against what we already decided to do.

He saddled the donkey before sunrise.

He rode out toward the money.

## The Sword in the Road

The Bible says God sent an angel with a drawn sword to stand in the way.

Balaam never saw it.

The donkey did.

She turned aside into a field. Balaam beat her.

She crushed his foot against a wall. Balaam beat her.

She lay down under him in the narrow place. Balaam beat her three times.

And then her mouth opened. What have I done unto thee, that thou hast smitten me these three times? Numbers 22:28.

That is not a children's story.

That is God stopping a man from killing himself.

## What the Donkey Saw

A man with a sword stood in front of him.

The angel said, behold, I went out to withstand thee, because thy way is perverse before me. And the ass saw me, and turned from me these three times. Unless she had turned from me, surely now also I had slain thee, and saved her alive.

Read that one again.

The donkey saved Balaam's life.

The thing he was beating was the thing keeping him breathing.

## You Have Beaten the Donkey

You lost the job you wanted.

You beat the donkey.

The woman said no when you were sure she was the one.

You beat the donkey.

The promotion went to somebody else. The house deal fell through. The check did not clear. The friend stopped calling. The door slammed shut. The body broke down right before the trip.

You beat the donkey.

You called it bad luck.

You called it warfare.

You called it the devil.

It was the angel.

## The Mercy You Cursed

I drove a garbage truck for years.

Every morning before light. Every winter in twenty below.

I thought God had forgotten where I lived.

I asked Him to let me out. He kept me there.

I beat that donkey for a decade.

Then He let me out at the right time, in the right way, with the right scars to write the right words to the right strangers at the right hour of the night.

The truck was the mercy.

The cold was the mercy.

The route was the mercy.

I did not see the sword. I only felt the wall against my foot.

## What Grace Looks Like When You Are Inside It

Grace looks like the deal that died.

Grace looks like the silence on the other end of the prayer.

Grace looks like the friend who stopped answering. The bank that said no. The body that gave out. The path that closed.

Grace looks like obstruction when you are inside it.

It only looks like mercy from the far side of the road.

## The Question Balaam Should Have Asked

Why is she stopping?

Three times she turned. Three times she warned him. Three times he answered with the staff.

If you have been hit three times in the same place, do not pick a fourth fight with the donkey.

Stop. Look up.

There is a sword in the road.

The closed door is not your enemy. It is your covering.

## The God Who Stands in the Way

The Lord is not slow concerning his promise.

He is slow because He is keeping you alive.

He stood in Balaam's path because He loved Israel and He loved Balaam. He did not want a curse on the people and He did not want a corpse on the donkey.

He stood in your path for the same reason.

The wall you keep slamming into is His hand.

You can keep beating the donkey.

Or you can fall on your face and say what Balaam finally said.

I have sinned, for I knew not that thou stoodest in the way against me.

That is the prayer that ends the story.

## The Last Thing

If a door slammed this week and you are still raw about it, hear this.

The donkey saved you.

You will not see the sword tonight. You may not see it for years.

But there is a sword. And there is a mercy with a drawn blade standing between you and a thing that would have killed you in slow motion.

Thou God seest me. He saw you turn into the field. He saw the wall. He saw the narrow place.

He sent the donkey on purpose.

Stop beating her.
