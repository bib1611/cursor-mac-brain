# The Cult You Cannot Leave Is the One You Built

You think you would have spotted it.

The robes. The compound. The smile that does not blink. The leader who calls himself God.

You would have walked out. You are sure.

But the cult you cannot leave is not down a dirt road in Waco. It is not in a bunker. It is not in a building with a sign.

It is the one you built around yourself.

You are the prophet. You are the elder. You are the lone member.

And you defend it like your life depends on it. Because in a way, it does.

---

I sat in a truck for ten years before God broke me. Diesel under my nails. Garbage in my hair.

I had every excuse polished and lined up.

Why I could not pray more. Why my Bible stayed shut. Why my temper was justified. Why the porn was not really the issue.

I had a doctrine for every sin.

That was the cult. I was the founding member. I was also the security guard at the door.

"The heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked: who can know it?" — Jeremiah 17:9

You cannot reform it. You cannot reason with it. You cannot vote it out.

You can only be raised from the dead.

---

Here is how you check yourself.

A cult cannot tolerate a question it has not pre-approved.

So ask your own soul a question it has not pre-approved.

When was the last time the Bible told you something you did not want to hear and you obeyed anyway?

If you cannot remember, you are not reading the Bible. You are reading yourself into it.

That is not faith. That is a mirror with verses taped on it.

---

The members of your inner cult have names.

Comfort. Pride. The story you tell yourself about why you are not the bad guy in your own marriage.

The version of God you invented because the real one made you flinch.

That last one is the leader.

He looks like Christ. He talks like Christ. He just never tells you no.

Paul saw him coming. "For if he that cometh preacheth another Jesus, whom we have not preached… ye might well bear with him." — 2 Corinthians 11:4

Another Jesus. A counterfeit. Yours.

---

The cult of self has a creed. Three lines.

I am basically a good man.
My situation is unique.
God understands.

Every member recites it without knowing the words. It runs underneath the day like a furnace.

It is why you keep clicking. Keep scrolling. Keep choosing the wife you have in your head over the one God gave you.

You think you are free. You are not.

A man who cannot tell himself no is not free. He is owned.

---

There is one way out.

It is not seven steps. It is not a journal. It is not a new app.

It is a man on a cross who said, "It is finished," and meant the cult of self too.

"I am crucified with Christ: nevertheless I live; yet not I, but Christ liveth in me." — Galatians 2:20

Not I.

Read that again. Slowly.

The founder dies. The lone member dies. The security guard dies.

Then a real man walks out.

---

Tonight ask the question you have been hiding from.

Not what you want God to say. What He has already said. Open the Book.

Read until something cuts.

Then obey it before sundown. Even if it costs you.

That is how the cult ends.

Not with a raid. Not with a documentary.

With a man finally telling himself the truth.

---
