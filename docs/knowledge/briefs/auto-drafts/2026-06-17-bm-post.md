# The Thing You Have Not Told Anybody

There is a thing in you nobody knows about.

You have carried it ten years.

You have carried it thirty.

You think the silence is keeping you safe.

The silence is killing you slow.

## What Silence Does to a Man

David said it in Psalm 32:3.

"When I kept silence, my bones waxed old through my roaring all the day long."

Read that line once more.

His bones got old. His body broke down. His teeth ached. His sleep died. His chest stayed tight from sunrise to sunrise.

That was the silence working.

Not the sin.

The silence about the sin.

You do not have to commit the act for the secret to rot you. You just have to keep your mouth shut about whatever happened to you.

## The Things Men Bury

A boy touched you when you were nine.

Your father told you you were stupid at the kitchen table and you believed him for forty years.

You looked at something on the screen you cannot unsee.

You hit her once and she forgave you and you never forgave yourself.

You walked away from the baby.

You took the money.

You called yourself a Christian and went to bed every night with the thing nobody knows.

That is the bones getting old.

## What I Carried for Thirty One Years

I was thirteen.

A man hurt me in a place that was supposed to keep me safe.

I told nobody.

Not my mother. Not my father. Not the pastor. Not the wife I would marry nine years later.

I carried it thirty one years and the bones got old.

I climbed in and out of garbage trucks at twenty below.

I sat on the train and watched the prairie go by and thought God had forgotten where I lived.

I did not know it was the silence.

I thought it was the world.

It was the thing I would not speak.

## Why You Are Still Tired

You wonder why you wake up exhausted.

You wonder why the prayer feels like talking to a ceiling.

You wonder why your wife says you are a million miles away in the same room.

You wonder why the small joys do not register anymore.

Your bones are old.

You are roaring all the day long and you do not even know it.

## What David Did

He kept reading.

Psalm 32:5.

"I acknowledged my sin unto thee, and mine iniquity have I not hid."

He stopped hiding.

He said the words out loud to the only One who already knew.

Then he wrote, "and thou forgavest the iniquity of my sin."

Forgavest.

Past tense.

The moment the secret left his mouth, the verdict was already done.

## The First Mouth

You are scared to tell a person because people talk.

I get that.

Start with the One who does not talk.

Close the door. Lock it. Get on your face on the carpet in the dark and say the thing out loud. The name of the person. The act. The year. The wound. The bitterness. The thing you did. The thing done to you.

Say it.

He already knows.

He is waiting for you to know that He knows.

## What Comes Next

Then find one man.

Not a small group. Not a podcast. Not a Facebook confessional booth.

One man with scars who knows how to hold a story without flinching.

James 5:16.

"Confess your faults one to another, and pray one for another, that ye may be healed."

That verse is not a suggestion.

It is a prescription.

Healing comes through the mouth of one man telling another man the thing he has been killing himself to keep quiet.

## The Lie That Keeps You Quiet

The devil whispers the same line to every man.

If they knew, they would leave.

If she knew, she would leave. If your kids knew, they would leave. If the church knew, they would leave. If God really knew, He would leave.

He already knows.

He has not left.

The thief comes to steal, kill, and destroy. He has been stealing your sleep with that whisper for two decades.

He is a liar and the father of it.

## What Happened to Me

I told my wife at forty four years old.

I told a man at a kitchen table the year after that.

I wrote it on the internet two years after that.

The bones started to soften.

The sleep came back in pieces.

The prayer started to land.

The wife who I thought would leave is still here, holding my hand harder than she ever has.

The thing I was sure would destroy my life is the thing that started my writing, the thing that pays my mortgage, the thing that built this stack you are reading right now.

The mercy was on the far side of the mouth.

## The Last Thing

There is a sentence sitting in your chest right now.

You know exactly what it is.

You have known for a year, ten years, thirty.

Say it tonight.

To Him first. To one trusted man this week.

The bones will start to come back.

Selah, said David.

Pause. Think on that.

He kept silence. The bones waxed old. He spoke. The iniquity was forgiven.

You are one sentence away from the same thing.
