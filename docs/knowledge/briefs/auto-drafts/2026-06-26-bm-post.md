# the people who hurt you wore the right clothes

You stopped going because of a person.

Not because of God. A person.

Someone who carried a Bible and used it like a club. Someone who smiled on Sunday and cut you on Tuesday. Right words, cold heart.

You met religion and called it God. They are not the same thing.

I know the taste of it. People who quote the verse and miss the man standing in front of them. People who can win the argument and lose the soul.

It leaves a mark. You walk out of the building and you think you walked out on Him.

You didn't. You walked out on them.

Here is what nobody tells the wounded. Jesus had a bad experience with religious people too.

The most religious men of His day watched Him heal and called it the devil. They knew the scriptures cold. They tithed the mint. They prayed loud on the corner. And they nailed God to a tree.

"He came unto his own, and his own received him not."

The hands that killed Him knew every verse.

So when religion burns you, you are not far from Christ. You are standing right where He stood. Mocked by the people who should have known better.

Don't let the men who wounded you become the reason you bury the God who didn't.

The Pharisee is not the standard. The cross is.

He was hurt by religious people first. And He still stretched out His arms on that hill and stayed there.

"Come unto me, all ye that labour and are heavy laden, and I will give you rest."

The door was never the problem. Some of the doormen were.

Walk past them.

He is still inside.

Come back. Not to them. To Him.
