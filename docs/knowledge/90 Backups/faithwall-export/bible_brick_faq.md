## A. Safety & Privacy

### What data do you collect?

Only what we absolutely need to make the app work. Your email (for account recovery), your verse progress (so we can sync across devices), and crash reports (so we can fix bugs). We don't collect your browsing history, your app usage details, or anything personally identifying beyond your login info. Think of it like this: we know you completed a verse, but we don't know what apps you were trying to open.

### Can you see what apps I use?

**No.** We can't see which apps you open, how long you use them, or what websites you visit. On iPhone, Bible Brick uses Apple's built-in Screen Time API — the blocking happens on your phone, not on our servers. On Android, we use accessibility services to detect when you're opening a blocked app, but that information never leaves your device. We're not Covenant Eyes — we're not here to monitor you. We're here to give you a gentle pause.

### Is my Bible reading data private?

**Absolutely.** Your verse history, prayer notes, streak counts, and any journal entries are tied to your account and encrypted in transit. We don't share this with anyone — not advertisers, not churches, not third parties. Your walk with God is between you and Him. We're just the tool that helps you get there.

### What about my kids' data? Are you COPPA compliant?

Yes, we take COPPA (the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act) very seriously. If your child is under 13, we require verifiable parental consent before collecting any data. We don't sell children's data, we don't use it for advertising, and we only keep what's necessary. Parents can review, delete, or request their child's data at any time — just email us. We collect as little as possible from kids because frankly, we don't want the liability, and it's the right thing to do.

### Can you lock me out permanently? What about emergency access?

Nope — you're always in control. We've built in "emergency unbricks" for those moments when you genuinely need access (medical emergency, urgent call, etc.). Plus, 911 and emergency calls always work — no exceptions. We also have a "grace period" setting where you can give yourself a few minutes of buffer time. We want this to be a helpful boundary, not a digital straitjacket.

## B. How It Works

### How does the app blocking actually work?

When you try to open a blocked app (like Instagram or TikTok), Bible Brick steps in and shows you a Bible verse instead. Read the verse, reflect on it, answer a simple reflection prompt, and then you're free to use your phone. It's like a speed bump for your soul — just enough time to ask yourself, "Is this really what I want to be doing right now?" On iPhone we use Apple's Screen Time API; on Android we use accessibility services. Both are official, secure methods.

### Will this slow down my phone?

Not noticeably. Bible Brick runs in the background using minimal resources — it's not constantly scanning or eating up battery. On iPhone especially, the Screen Time API is built by Apple to be lightweight. We hear this concern a lot (especially from parents whose kids' phones are already slow), so we've made performance a priority. If anything, your phone might feel faster because you're not bouncing between social media apps all day.

### Does it work when I'm offline?

**Yes!** This is one of our favorite features. We cache Bible verses locally on your device, so the brick works whether you're on a plane, at a cabin, or your WiFi is being spotty (again). Your streaks sync when you reconnect, but the core experience — reading a verse and unlocking your apps — works anywhere. Perfect for those family camping trips when you still want the kids to do their Bible Brick before asking for the tablet.

### What apps can it block? What about calls and texts?

You can block pretty much any distracting app: social media (Instagram, TikTok, Snapchat, Facebook), games, news apps, shopping (Amazon, Temu), YouTube, streaming — whatever tempts you most. Essential apps like Phone, Messages, your Bible app, Maps, Camera, and Calculator always remain available. Phone calls and texts are **never** blocked, and 911 emergency calls always go through — no exceptions. Bible Brick is a guardrail, not a roadblock.

### How do I set up different modes for different times?

You can create custom "Brick Modes" — like "School Time" (blocks games and social), "Family Dinner" (blocks everything except calls), "Bedtime" (blocks all entertainment apps), or "Sabbath" (full brick except Bible and phone). Set them on a schedule so they activate automatically, or toggle them manually. Parents tell us this is their favorite feature because once it's set up, it's one less thing to nag about.

## C. Bible Content

### What Bible translations do you use?

We include multiple translations so you can choose what speaks to you: ESV (great for study), NIV (familiar and accessible), NLT (easy to understand for kids), KJV (for the traditionalists), NKJV, CSB, and NASB. You can pick your default and even switch between translations for the same verse to get a fuller picture. Premium users get access to even more translations including Spanish (RVR1960, NVI), French (LSG), German (LUT), and Portuguese (NVI-PT).

### Are the verses random or themed?

Both! You can choose "Daily Bread" for a curated verse of the day selected by our team, or pick themed collections like "Anxiety & Peace," "Parenting with Grace," "Strength for Today," "Patience," "Wisdom," or "Joy." We also have seasonal collections — Advent, Lent, back-to-school, summer break. If your teen is going through something specific, there's probably a collection for it. The verses aren't random — they're chosen to meet you where you are.

### Can I add my own verses?

Yes! Premium users can create custom verse collections. Many homeschool moms love this for memorization — add the weekly memory verse, a passage your family is studying, or verses that speak to a particular struggle your child is facing. You can even record your own prayer prompts. We love seeing families create their own "Family Scripture" collection that everyone works through together.

### Do I have to be a Christian to use this? Is this affiliated with a specific church?

You don't have to be a Christian to use Bible Brick. While we built this for Christians, we've had non-Christian users tell us they love the app because it forces a moment of mindfulness before mindless scrolling. And no — we're not affiliated with any church or denomination. Our team includes Christians from various backgrounds, and we're committed to keeping the focus on God's Word rather than any particular tradition. No judgment, no preaching beyond the text itself.

## D. Family & Kids

### Can I set this up on my kids' phones and control it from mine?

Yes, and honestly, this is where Bible Brick shines. You install it on your child's device and manage everything from your own phone through our Family Dashboard — block apps, set schedules, see streak progress, and send encouraging notes. It's not spy-level monitoring; your kids know what you're seeing. The goal is to build trust and gradually hand over control as they develop their own discipline. It's like giving your kids training wheels for self-control.

### Does this work for teens, or will they just uninstall it?

Here's the honest truth: a determined teen can bypass almost any app blocker (research shows the average 15-year-old can find a workaround in under 5 minutes). That's why we built "Strict Mode" — when enabled, it requires a parent passcode to uninstall or change settings, and bypass attempts are logged. But more importantly, we designed this to be something teens *want* to use. The streak system, personalized verses, and sense of accomplishment resonate with many teens. The goal isn't a perfect lockdown; it's a conversation starter and a habit builder.

### What age is appropriate?

We've seen it work well for kids as young as 8 (with heavy parent involvement) up through adults. For younger kids (8-10), start with shorter brick sessions and do the verses together. For tweens (11-13), they're usually ready for more independence. For teens (14-18), it works best when they have a say in their own block lists and schedules. Treat it like any other tool: start with more oversight, then gradually release control.

### Can my spouse and I share a family plan? Can kids see my progress?

Absolutely! Our Family Plan covers up to 6 family members — you, your spouse, and up to 4 kids. Everyone gets their own profile, streaks, and block lists. And yes, through our optional Family Wall, your kids can see your progress too. When you share your streak ("Mom's on a 45-day streak!"), it creates gentle accountability and turns discipline into family culture. The Wall is completely optional, and you control what gets shared. When your kids see *you* doing your Bible Brick every day, they're way more likely to buy in.

### What if my teenager refuses to use it?

This is a heart issue, not a tech issue — and we say that with all the gentleness of a mom who's been there. If your teen is outright refusing, forcing the issue will likely backfire. Our suggestion? Start with yourself. Let them see you doing your Bible Brick every morning. Share a verse that spoke to you. When they see it's making a real difference in your life (less stress, more patience, genuinely less phone time), curiosity often follows. Some parents have told us their teens eventually asked to install it *because* they saw the change. You can't control their hearts, but you can model what you want to see.

## F. Technical Issues

### Which phones does this work on? iPhone vs. Android?

Bible Brick works on iPhones running iOS 16 and newer, and Android phones running Android 10 and newer. The iPhone version tends to be a bit smoother because Apple's Screen Time API gives us more reliable blocking. The Android version works great too but requires enabling accessibility services (don't worry — we walk you through it). We don't currently support tablets, but we're working on it!

### Will this drain my battery?

Minimal impact. Bible Brick doesn't run constantly in the background — it only activates when you try to open a blocked app. On iPhone, the Screen Time API handles most of the work, which is very battery-efficient. On Android, we use about 1-2% of daily battery in our tests. If anything, your phone might feel like it lasts longer because you're not bouncing between social media apps all day.

### Why does it need so many permissions?

Here's what we ask for and why:
- **Accessibility Service (Android only):** Lets us detect when you're opening a blocked app so we can show the verse. We don't read your screen or spy on you.
- **Screen Time API (iPhone):** Apple's official method for app management. It only tells us "this app was opened," not what you did in it.
- **Notifications:** So we can send gentle reminder nudges (only if you enable them).
- **Internet:** To sync your account and load new verses.

That's it. No contacts, no camera, no microphone, no location. We ask for the minimum.

### How do I uninstall if I don't like it?

We hope you'll love Bible Brick, but if it's not for you, uninstalling is straightforward. On iPhone: delete the app, then go to Settings > Screen Time and remove any lingering restrictions. On Android: disable the accessibility service first (Settings > Accessibility), then uninstall. If you're in Strict Mode, you'll need the parent passcode. Detailed instructions are in our Support Center, and we're always happy to walk you through it.

## G. The Hard Questions

### Isn't this just replacing one addiction with another? Scrolling the Bible app instead of Instagram?

That's a really thoughtful question, and one we've wrestled with ourselves. Here's our take: there's a fundamental difference between mindlessly scrolling an algorithmic feed designed to hijack your dopamine system, and intentionally pausing to read Scripture. Bible Brick isn't about keeping you on your phone longer — it's about creating a sacred pause that disrupts the habit loop. Most users tell us they actually spend *less* time on their phones overall, not more. But you're right to ask — if you find yourself racing through verses just to get back to scrolling, that might be a heart check moment.

### How is this different from just using Apple's Screen Time or Google's Digital Wellbeing?

Screen Time and Digital Wellbeing are great tools — we actually built Bible Brick to work alongside them. But they have limitations: they're purely time-based ("you've used Instagram for 30 minutes") and easy to override with one tap. Bible Brick adds the *soul* component. Instead of a cold timer, you get a moment with Scripture. Instead of just saying "no," you're invited into something better. Many users combine both: Screen Time for the hard limits, Bible Brick for the meaningful pause.

### Why would I pay for this when I can just delete the apps?

If you can delete Instagram and never look back, God bless you — seriously! For some people, cold turkey works. But for most of us (especially our kids), these apps are embedded in school communication, church youth group chats, family group texts, and social connection. Deleting them entirely cuts off healthy relationships along with the unhealthy ones. Bible Brick lets you keep the apps you genuinely need while building the discipline to use them intentionally. It's a middle path — and for many families, it's more sustainable than all-or-nothing.

### Doesn't this make me dependent on an app for self-control?

Ideally? No. Realistically? Maybe temporarily — and that's okay. Think of it like training wheels. When your child is learning to ride a bike, the training wheels aren't the goal; the goal is the skill they build while using them. Most long-term Bible Brick users tell us they eventually need the app less and less. The habit of pausing and praying becomes internalized. But while you're building that muscle, there's no shame in using a tool. "The wise store up knowledge" (Proverbs 10:14). Using wisdom to structure your environment is, well, wisdom.

### What if my kids see me bypassing the Bible task?

Oof. This one hits home, doesn't it? Here's the truth: kids are always watching, and they're way more observant than we give them credit for. If you're setting up Bible Brick on your child's phone but bypassing it on your own, they'll notice — and it will undermine everything. That's one of the reasons we built the Family Wall. When you share your streak with your family, it creates gentle accountability. Our advice? Start with yourself. Get your own streak going. Let them see you struggle and succeed. Modeling is the most powerful parenting tool you have — way more powerful than any app.
