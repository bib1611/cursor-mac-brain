> Thank you, brother. I am so tired of men who say they are King James Bible believers and then try to go to "the Greek" (or Hebrew) to prove what they want to teach. They claim the KJB is God's perfect, preserved words and then they substitute other words that a lexicon suggests the greek or hebrew word could be translated as instead of just believing and keeping the word God preserved and gave us. A good read and eye-opening for me was the book by Gustavus Paine "The Men Behind the KJV." The company of men (around 50 of them) who met and finalized the 1611 were some of the most educated and elite scholars of their day, had command of multiple languages, were intimate scholars of Greek and Hebrew, and had access to manuscripts and fragments that have long since disappeared.
> 
> I have no quarrel with people who have been "educated" out of believing God has preserved his word in the KJB...I was one of them for several years. Not anymore. Every one of us will have to give account to God. I'd dread having to answer to God why I diluted and corrupted His word.
> 
> Another old book's title says it best: "Things That Are Different Are Not the Same." I'm keeping my King James Bible.
> 
> You are correct when you say the serpent still speaks the same doubts into people's minds today: "Yea, hath God said?" That's the basis for all newer translations (along with the sales generated from all the study guides and concordances that accompany them). There's a reason why Psalms 12:6-7 and 2 Timothy 2:15 read the way they do in the King James Bible and practically no other. I believe the 1611 KJB is the word of God. I also believe that the translations since are what men wished God had said.
